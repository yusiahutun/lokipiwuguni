Drake wears a Jeezy T-shirt at after-party for LA tour stop... as some fans express 'disappointment'

Drake was seen showing off his personal style as he arrived to an after-party in West Hollywood following his L.A. concert for the It's All A Blue Tour on Tuesday. 

The One Dance hitmaker, 36, who has recently fueled romance rumors with rapper Sexxy Red, will conclude his four-night stop in Inglewood at the Kia Forum on Wednesday.

As he arrived at the late night bash, the Grammy winner donned a black, short-sleeved graphic T-shit for Can't Ban The Snowman - a nod to Young Jeezy - as well as a pair of baggy, leather pants. 

He slipped into a pair of white shoes with chunky soles and easily carried his smartphone in his hand while making his way through a crowded sidewalk. 

This sighting comes as some fans have jumped to social media to express their negative opinions about the show, with some claiming they 'left early.' 

However, many A list stars like Kim Kardashian and Kendall Jenner seem to have had fun at the show.

Drake looked ready to party.

The star accessorized his look with a pair of small, rhinestone earrings for a flashy touch, as well as a few bracelets placed on his right wrist. 

Drake kicked off the It's All A Blue Tour, which is co-headlined by 21 Savage, last month in Chicago to promote their latest collaborative album titled, Her Loss. The tour is slated to come to an end later this fall on October 9 in Columbus, Ohio. 

Fans of the singer have recently took to social media to express their feelings towards some of his concerts. One social media user, named Nadia Soffer, shared a video on TikTok to respond to a comment that read, 'DUDE I LITERALLY LEFT EARLY FOR THIS REASON.'

The fan explained, 'He's really out there doing the most, but actually doing the least, if that makes sense.

'Like, he sings like 30 songs, but probably like 30 seconds of each song and then the part of the song that he sang, he was out there doing I don't even know what,' and added that he is 'sometimes making up new lyrics.

'Or he's singing the lyrics but offbeat...I love Drake. He's my top person on Spotify. I spoke about this man in my wedding vows 'cause my husband loves Drake so much. And this was my first time seeing him and I just was, like, super bummed,' she admitted. 

'Like, I'm here to see you live, to see you actually sing and rap your music, and I don't even know what you were doing. And then 21 Savage comes on in the middle, and the vibe drops super down.

'And at the end, he [Drake] sings like two full songs, which was the best thing ever. But, like, that was it.'

Other fans flocked to the comment section on the nearly one-minute video, with one penning, 'He pulled a Frank Ocean on y'all,' referencing to the singer's set at Coachella in April that left fans less than pleased. 

Another wrote, 'I paid so much money for those tickets and then he's like throwing the mic out for us to sing like whatttt,' with the uploader responding with, 'Like I paid for you to sing not all these people.'

One revealed, 'So glad I paid $100 for the tickets. I left early too lmao,' while another defended the star. 'NY wasn't like this lol. Bro was rapping/singing bar for bar.' 

However, a TikTok user expressed, 'This was me in DC and i felt so disappointed,' with another typing out, 'Dude I was tryna sing and he wasn't playing the way the songs are.' 

Nadia also posted a shorter clip that was taken from the concert while he was performing live on stage and added the text, 'Just waiting for Drake to sing at least one of his songs how he actually recorded them so I can sing along...' 

The comment section was once again filled with other concertgoers who had a similar experience. 'Finally someone said it!!! great concert but I felt like I was singing the wrong lyrics or i was too early or too late.' 

Another fan humorously stated, 'I've been telling everyone that when i was at the concert it felt like being in the car w someone who skips a song after playing it for 20 seconds.' 

One admitted that they had changed their minds about attending his upcoming concert. 'Sold my $250 ticket for $400 today. I've seen enough videos to know I don't need to be in the nosebleeds for this.'

Over the weekend on Sunday, Kim Kardashian attended Drake's concert and looked back on her past while attending the show. 

The reality TV icon was joined by her sister Kendall Jenner's boyfriend Bad Bunny in clips from the show that were shared on social media, and at one point she was seen quoting herself in response to a sample featured on one of Drake's song.

The single, Search & Rescue, was released in April and may be one of the songs featured on his upcoming studio LP For All The Dogs, which is expected to be released in September.

In video from the audience, Kim could be seen smiling and shouting out her now-iconic lines, which Drake sampled from the 2021 series finale of Keeping Up With The Kardashians, before Kim and her family began their current Hulu reality series The Kardashians. 

Drake has previously had a contentious relationship with Kim's ex-husband Kanye West, but the reality star made it clear that she didn't have any hard feelings for the hitmaker after she was seen at his show's afterparty.

In clips of her meet-up with Bad Bunny on the floor of The Forum in Inglewood, Kim can be seen in a sizzling gray bodycon stress with short sleeves.

Although she is initially drained out by noise from the stage, she can later be heard as she playfully shouts louder to Bad Bunny. 'I didn't come this far,' she screams, 'just to come this far and not be happy!'

It was a completely different delivery for a line that she previously said to her mother Kris Jenner on the KUWTK finale.

As the mother of four went viral as she jammed to her sample, her younger sister Kendall and Puerto Rican rapper, Bad Bunny, also drew some attention during the festivities. 

The pair were seen being very affectionate with each other, with the model gently caressing his face while the performer wrapped his arm around her waist. 

The two were first linked earlier this year in February but have not publicly commented on the status of their relationship.
