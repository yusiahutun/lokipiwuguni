'Killer' who 'helped kidnap and murder Turkish radio DJ' tells court he did not see the body when he

A 'killer' who 'helped kidnap and murder' a Turkish radio DJ told the court that he did not see the body when he helped get rid of it because he had his eyes shut. 

The body of DJ Koray Alpergin, 43, was discovered near the Oakwood Hill Industrial Estate in Loughton, Essex, on 15 October last year after he had been tortured to death. 

Ali Kavak, 26, said he had been asked to drive vehicles and wait near Mr Alpergin's home but did not know why, before he was asked to help move the body after the horrific killing. 

Giving evidence, Kavak said he didn't see the body when he moved it from vehicle to vehicle on the way to Essex because he had his eyes shut, and claimed he wasn't thinking straight as his girlfriend had recently broken up with him and moved abroad. 

Mr Alpergin, from northern Cyprus, was the owner of Turkish radio station Bizim FM and had connections with celebrities such as rapper P Diddy and internet sensation 'Salt Bae'.

Mr Alpergin was beaten with a baseball bat, had boiling water was poured on him and endured other horrific torture methods used

He was kidnapped from outside his flat in Enfield with girlfriend Gozde Dalbudak, 34, and they were 'frog-marched' into a white van after arriving home from an expensive Italian restaurant in Mayfair, the Old Bailey heard.

Mr Alpergin was beaten with a baseball bat, had boiling water poured over him and endured other horrific torture methods while Ms Dalbudak was locked in a toilet for two days, jurors were told.

The naked body of Mr Alpergin was then dumped in the woodland and the vehicles used set alight before being found by a dog walker, the court heard.

 Accomplices Steffan Gordon, 34, Tejean Kennedy, 33, Samuel Owusu-Opuku, 35, Junior Kettle, 32, and Ali Kavak, 26, all deny murder and false imprisonment.

Kennedy, Owusu-Opuku, Kettle and Kavak also deny kidnapping Mr Alpergin and Ms Dalbudak on 13 October 2022, while Gordon admits it.

Kavak and Erdogan Ulcay, 56, deny perverting the course of justice by assisting with the disposal of Mr Alpergin's body or the destruction by fire of a Fiat Diablo van and Renault Megane.

'Why did you think you were outside Mr Alpergin's address?' prosecutor Peter Ratliff asked Kavak.

Kavak said: 'I didn't know I was outside his home address.

'I thought I was running errands. I didn't question it. I was told to drive there. It became something normal. I was just told to drive and did as I was told.'

He said the other defendants would talk in 'unclear short sentences' and have 'coded conversations'.

Mr Ratliff said: 'People do all sorts of things after a break up. Some people drink or send flowers or write bad poetry.

'Your case seems to be that heartbreak lead you to accidentally join an international crime group.

'This break up didn't make you stupid did it?

Mr Alpergin, from northern Cyprus, was the owner of Turkish radio station Bizim FM and had connections with celebrities such as rapper P Diddy

'It made me vulnerable,' Kavak replied.

'Do you agree you are not an idiot?' Mr Ratliff asked.

'No but I would say that I was gullible,' Kavak replied.

'You are I suggest an intelligent and articulate man, do you agree?' Mr Ratliff said.

'Not quite no,' Kavak replied.

Mr Ratliff said Kavak had visited the 'killing ground', the warehouse where Mr Alpergin was killed, five times.

DJ Koray Alpergin, 43, had celebrity connections including internet sensation 'Salt Bae'

Kavak said he was told to take a vehicle to marshland and dispose of it after moving the body.

Kavak said he held back in his police interview because at the time 'he felt scared and threatened and 'didn't know what these people were capable of, the fact someone had lost their life in a horrific way.'

Kavak told the court that Kennedy had made death threats against him and his family if he named him when giving evidence in court.

He said three weeks earlier, Kennedy approached him in the Belmarsh jail exercise yard and told him he had guns, money and loyal friends outside prison, and that they harm the families of anyone who spoke against him.

Kavak said Kennedy told him 'he has lots of young boys outside and lots of firearms and lots of money and would make sure that all of the young boys that love him will go and harm somebody's family.'

Girlfriend Gozde Dalbudak, 34, was locked in a toilet for two days wile Mr Alpergin endured horrific torture

He said Kennedy added that 'it would be easy to find someone who lives in Tottenham', and that he 'should be sure he doesn't mention my name'.

Kavak's family lives in the Tottenham area, so he took this as a direct threat against them.

He also told the court that a couple of weeks earlier in the dock, Kettle handed him a note that he understood had been written by Kennedy.

He was asked to read the note to the court: 'My man got Ps on you. Looks like I got to do the mad ting too.'

The barrister asked: 'What did that mean?'

'A bounty had been put on myself', Kavak replied.

Jurors had heard that 'P' meant 'pounds'.

Asked by the barrister how he knew Kennedy had written it, he said 'previously he had threatened me and co-defendants.

'He had told me that Steffan Gordon, when he does go into the witness box to give evidence, if he does mention my name he will never see his kid again..'

Asked what the 'mad ting' meant Kavak explained that it referred to mentioning Kennedy's name, which, he said, is 'something that cannot be done, it's crazy.'

Kavak was then asked directly whether he had anything to do with the kidnap or murder of Mr Alpergin, or the kidnapping of Ms Dalbudak.

Giving evidence, Kavak said he didn't see the body when he moved it from vehicle to vehicle on the way to Essex because he had his eyes shut

'No, not at all,' he replied.

He insisted that his only involvement related to driving one of the vehicles used in the incident, and to helping dispose of the body.

When asked why, if he wasn't involved in the kidnapping or murder, he hadn't gone to the police he said: 'They knew where my family lived and I was unable to cross the line.

'The only thing on my mind was to do as I was told.'

The court also heard members of the public had tried to to communicate with defendants from the public gallery.

Judge Sarah Whitehouse KC opened proceedings by reminding those in the public gallery that they were not allowed to communicate with anyone in the courtroom in any way, shape or form.

She said that if anybody breached this order she 'will not hesitate to ban people from sitting in the public gallery for the duration of the trial.'

She added: 'I do understand that feelings can run high, but this is a court room and not a bar.'

Kennedy, of Cricklewood Broadway, Cricklewood, Owusu-Opuku, of Dunbar Road, Wood Green, Kettle, of Kingsdown Road, Islington, and Kavak, of De Quincey Road, Tottenham, deny murder and two counts of kidnap and false imprisonment.

Gordon, of Dehavilland Close, Northolt, denies murder and two counts of false imprisonment.

Ulcay, of Oakeshott Avenue, Camden and Kavak deny preventing the course of justice.

The trial continues.
