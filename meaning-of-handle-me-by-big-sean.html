Meaning of Handle Me by Big Sean

The overall theme of the song "Handle Me" by Big Sean revolves around personal growth, resilience, and the ability to overcome obstacles in life. Throughout the track, Big Sean reflects on his journey and how he navigates challenges to reach success. The lyrics delve into various aspects of his life, highlighting significant moments and experiences that contribute to his growth.One standout lyric that further develops the theme is: "I'm talking pure addition, that's my pure addiction, something out of nothing, we were not magicians." Here, Big Sean emphasizes his dedication to progress and the relentless pursuit of success. He acknowledges that he starts from humble beginnings and doesn't possess extraordinary powers but instead relies on hard work and dedication to create something out of nothing. This theme of perseverance and determination resonates with listeners, inspiring them to strive for their goals despite the odds.Another important lyric that adds depth to the song's theme is: "Back when momma had bills, back when life was heavy as an anvil." This lyric highlights the hardships Big Sean and his family faced during his upbringing. It symbolizes the weight of responsibility and financial burdens that influenced his drive to succeed. It contributes to the narrative of overcoming adversity and showcases the importance of resilience and determination in one's journey towards success.Furthermore, the lyric "Back when stepbro used to move the pills, I thought it was an Advil" delves into a darker side of Big Sean's past. While discussing the experiences he encountered, he alludes to his stepbrother's involvement in drug dealing. This theme of overcoming negative influences and choosing a different path emphasizes personal growth and the ability to rise above circumstances. It serves as a reminder that one's past does not define their future and that individuals have the power to change their narrative.The lyric "What they never handed me, I'm just a young living out the fantasies" emphasizes self-reliance and pursuing one's dreams against all odds. It captures the sentiment of taking control of one's destiny and not relying on others to create opportunities. It promotes the idea that success is within one's own hands, and it is up to individuals to work hard and make their dreams a reality.Overall, the song "Handle Me" encapsulates Big Sean's journey towards success, shedding light on the challenges he faced and overcame along the way. Through different lyrics, the song explores themes of resilience, personal growth, and self-reliance. It encourages listeners to believe in themselves, stay focused, and persist through obstacles, ultimately inspiring them on their own paths towards success.
