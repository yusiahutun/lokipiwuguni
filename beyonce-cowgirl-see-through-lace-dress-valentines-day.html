Beyonc Rides into Her Cowgirl Era in a See-Through Lace Dress

This ain’t Texas, but Beyoncé is still paying homage to her home state through her fashion choices.

In a new video posted to Instagram on Wednesday night, the superstar showed off one of likely many future cowgirl-themed outfits as she revs up to release Renaissance Act II, her first-ever full-length country album.

The ensemble consisted of a lacy black see-through dress from Dolce & Gabbana in a polka-dot pattern, with a corseted bodice and ruffled skirt. She drove home the country-western theme with a black leather garter, a black cowboy hat worn atop a matching lace veil, and turquoise jewelry stamped onto her nails. To finish off the look, she wore see-through black mesh opera gloves and black pointed-toe pumps.

And of course, since it was Valentine’s Day, husband Jay-Z joined in on the fashion fun. He posed with his wife in one photo, looking suave in a classic black tuxedo worn with a white dress shirt.

Beyoncé has been teasing Act II for some time now, wearing a cowboy hat to the Grammys earlier this month before officially announcing the project in a surprise Verizon commercial that aired during last weekend’s Super Bowl. Two singles from Act II, due out March 29, are currently available to stream: “Texas Hold ’Em” and “16 Carriages.”

“I feel a renaissance emerging, and I want to be part of nurturing that escape in any way possible,” the singer said in an interview for Harper’s Bazaar’s September 2021 cover story. “I’ve been in the studio for a year and a half. Sometimes it takes a year for me to personally search through thousands of sounds to find just the right kick or snare. One chorus can have up to 200 stacked harmonies. Still, there’s nothing like the amount of love, passion, and healing that I feel in the recording studio. After 31 years, it feels just as exciting as it did when I was nine years old. Yes, the music is coming!”
