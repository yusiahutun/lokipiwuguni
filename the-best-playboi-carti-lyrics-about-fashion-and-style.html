The best Playboi Carti lyrics about Fashion and style

Fashion and style often serve as an integral part of an artist's persona, providing a unique avenue for self-expression and creativity. In the realm of hip hop, Playboi Carti stands out as an individual who effortlessly embodies this concept. With his distinct musical style and fashion choices, Carti has established himself as a trendsetter within the music industry, solidifying his influence not only in the realm of music but also in the world of fashion.One aspect of Carti's fashion sense that makes him an intriguing figure is his ability to blend both high fashion and streetwear effortlessly. In his song "Magnolia," Carti showcases this unique style, rapping, "In New York, I milly rock, hide it in my sock / Running from an opp, then I shoot at opp / And I'm on the block, and I'm on the block / In New York, I milly rock, hide it in my sock." In these lyrics, Carti highlights his fashion choices by mentioning the famous "milly rock" moves, which have become synonymous with urban style. By referencing this popular dance move, Carti is not only highlighting his fashion choices but also inviting his audience to join in on the trend.Furthermore, Carti's lyrics demonstrate his deep love for designer brands and his desire to embrace luxury fashion. In the track "Wokeuplikethis*," featuring Lil Uzi Vert, Carti raps, "Brand new whip, just hopped in / I got options, I could pass that bitch like Stockton." These lyrics showcase Carti's passion for high-end cars as a symbol of his success and style. By name-dropping John Stockton, an NBA legend known for his precision and style of play, Carti cleverly links his own style to the basketball icon's on-court finesse. This fusion of music and fashion demonstrates his dedication to expressing himself through a combination of art forms.Additionally, Carti's lyrics often highlight his affinity for fashionable accessories, such as jewelry and designer garments. In his song "Fetti," featuring Maxo Kream, Carti raps, "Balenciaga, I'm draped in it, Raf Simons, I ain't wearin' it / Four pockets filled with racks all by Ksubi, I ain't sharin' it." These lyrics underscore Carti's appreciation for high-end brands and his innate sense of style. Referencing Balenciaga and Raf Simons, two renowned fashion houses, speaks to his dedication to fashion with a penchant for name-dropping iconic designers. Furthermore, Carti's mention of Ksubi, an Australian streetwear brand, reflects his ability to seamlessly incorporate diverse fashion influences into his personal style.Carti's fashion choices also extend beyond clothing, with his interest in unique hairstyles and accessories becoming a defining part of his image. In the song "Lookin," Carti proclaims, "And I got long hair, Chanel, that's invite only / I be rockin' Raf, too." Here, Carti showcases his individuality by describing his long hair, which has become a signature element of his overall style. By incorporating designer brands like Chanel and Raf Simons into his lyrics, Carti reinforces his status as a fashion icon, emphasizing his dedication to pushing boundaries in his artistic expression.In conclusion, Playboi Carti's lyrics reveal a strong connection to the theme of fashion and style. By blending both high fashion and streetwear effortlessly, Carti establishes himself as a trendsetter within the music industry. His appreciation for designer brands, accessories, and unique hairstyles further solidifies his status as a fashion icon. Through his lyrics, Carti showcases his love for fashion and his commitment to incorporating it as a defining element of his artistry. From his choice of clothing and accessories to his expressive hairstyles, Playboi Carti effortlessly captivates audiences with his distinct fashion sense, leaving an indelible mark on both the music and fashion industries.
