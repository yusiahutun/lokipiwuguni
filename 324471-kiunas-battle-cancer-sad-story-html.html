Kiuna's battle with cancer - the sad story

Bishop Allan Kiuna and Reverend Kathy Kiuna are an iconic marriage couple who, despite their humble beginnings, rose to fame by spreading the word of God. Bishop Allan Kiuna is the chief founder of Jubilee Christian Church, and his wife, Kathy, works under him. She is not only a preacher, a gospel artist, and author but also the founder of Daughters of Zion. Lately, Bishop Kiuna's sickness has been the talk of the town.

We all know several people in our families or renowned people who have been battling cancer, some succumbing to the illness while others overcoming and becoming survivors. Recently, through a lengthy Instagram post, we got to know about the challenging moments that Kathy Kiuna has been going through for the past year. But what do you know about the Kiuna cancer story?

Does Bishop Allan Kiuna have cancer?

In July 2019, Bishop Allan Kiuna broke the news that he was suffering from cancer. This was when he sent his condolences to the families of politicians Joyce Laboso and Ken Okoth who succumbed to the illness. Nevertheless, he did not share which type of cancer he had. His exact words were:

"Am so grateful to God for having seen me through a difficult battle with cancer last year. My heart goes out to the families of all who have Lost their loved ones to the scourge. We are praying for God’s strength in your hour of grief. May God comfort the family of Gov. Laboso. And May God comfort the family of Hon. Ken Okoth. We are praying for you for God’s grace in your hour of grief.”

Last week, Rev Kathy Kiuna shared with her fans on Instagram concerning the most challenging journey of her life. In the post, Kathy announced that her husband Allan was diagnosed with cancer in 2018.

According to the televangelist, chemotherapy was her husband's biggest blow due to its adverse effects. Because of the treatment, Allan became weak, lost his hair, gained weight, and was incapable of standing or walking for long. Due to this, Pastor Kiuna could not perform any of his responsibilities in the church.

Rev Kathy Kiuna steps in for her husband

While Allan was away getting treatment, Rev Kathy Kiuna had to step in as the head of the church. According to Kathy, it was quite tough to stand and address the congregation instead of being with her ailing spouse. Reverend Kathy further clarified that most of the topics she preached in 2018 were to strengthen her during that difficult moment. She believes that the Almighty was using her to assist others that were going through the same or worse.

Despite how Rev Kathy felt, she had to be strong and show Allan that God's faithfulness would heal him.

Allan Kiuna recovery

Towards the end of the long Instagram post, Kathy expressed that Bishop Kiuna was feeling much better and is doing well physically. She even posted various images showing the bishop's transition from when he was weak and losing hair to how he looks presently.

According to the post, Kathy explains that the first and second images were taken when Allan was undergoing chemotherapy. The third picture was taken when the bishop was released from the hospital. In the fourth picture, one can see the bishop in his church preaching, although he was wearing a hat and could not stand for long. The last picture showed a healthier bishop Kiuna in action during the 40 Days of Glory campaign organized by the church.

According to Kathy, Bishop Kiuna's hair has started to grow back; he is physically fit, can run, jump, and stand for hours. The reverend believes that with God, all things are possible, and she could not be happier. She thanks God for restoring Bishop Allan Kiuna's health.

The battle with cancer is never an easy one, and it affects both the ailing and their close ones. Reverend Kathy Kiuna posted her story as a motivation to others going through challenging times. She believes that God allowed Allan to go through his predicament to make him stronger so that he can bring healing to others by praying for them. When you have faith in God, then you can overcome anything that life throws at you, Kathy concluded.
