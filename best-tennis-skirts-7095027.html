The 14 Best Tennis Skirts, According to Editors &amp; Stylists

Tenniscore is the trend that may never die, and rightfully so. Since 2020, preppy style has been reintroduced to the zeitgeist. Over time, it has continued to evolve and remains a staple in many of our wardrobes. The key to tenniscore? Tennis skirts, of course. Even if you’ve never picked up a racket, the classic tennis skirt is a stylish look from the court to the coffee shop.

“The number one thing to finding a good tennis skirt is paying attention to the waistband and making sure the silhouette of the skirt matches with your body,” says style blogger Akia Danielle. 

When choosing our favorites, we stuck with technical skirts, meaning they were designed for tennis or some kind of athletic activity, but can also be styled to wear casually.

Best Overall

NikeCourt Dri-Fit Victory Tennis Skirt

Nike

What We Love: While the regular size range is pretty expansive, Nike also offers this skirt in plus-sizes.

What We Don’t Love: The built-in undershorts sometimes peek through from under the skirt.

We’ll admit there’s no such thing as the perfect tennis skirt, but the NikeCourt Dri-Fit Victory Skirt comes close, thanks to its sustainable makeup, fair price, versatility, and (our favorite aspect) the inclusive sizing. 

Made from over 75 percent recycled polyester, the light and breezy fabric is supremely stretchy and features Nike’s signature Dri-Fit technology that wicks sweat away. Whether you’re serving on the court or strolling around town, you’ll be able to move freely because of the skirt’s technical design. The single pleat in the front creates additional space for your legs (and offers a more traditional tennis look), while the built-in shorts provide ample coverage and slight compression. Plus, you can even securely store a ball or two beneath the hem. And while the shorts do run a bit long (they can sometimes show from under the skirt), we don’t think it affects the overall sporty look. 

Size: XS–2XL | Material: Recycled polyester | Colors: 4

Best Budget

Fulbelle Tennis Skirt

Amazon

What We Love: While many tennis skirts have a pocket along the inner shorts, this one also has two on the outer skirt layer, perfect for storing your phone and wallet.

What We Don’t Love: The fabric is very thin, and if you select a lighter color, it can look slightly see through.

The tenniscore trend shows no signs of slowing down, but if you’re still unsure whether or not you fully want to lean into the aesthetic, it’s better to ease into the style with an affordable item. At less than $30, this pick from Fulbelle doesn’t skimp on quality or style. In fact, it has several exceptional features that skirts triple the price don’t have. Namely, the addition of pockets on the outer skirt. 

It’s very common for tennis skirts to have a stretchy compartment sewn into the undershorts, but because this style has pockets located on the outer layer, you can easily reach your phone or wallet. (And you could even store a ball or two if need be.) 

Pockets aside, we also appreciate that the wide waistband includes a hidden inner drawcord to customize the fit for your body. And you won’t catch yourself adjusting the shorts either — the silicone strips sewn into the hem prevent rolling. 

Looks-wise, the skirt comes in both solid colors and playful patterns for those who like to make a statement. But be aware that when you select a lighter hue, including white, the undershorts can look slightly see through, so plan your undergarments accordingly. 

Size: S–2XL | Material: Polyester, spandex | Colors: 12

Best Splurge

Tory Burch Pleated Laser Cut Tennis Skirt

Shopbop

What We Love: The laser-cut flower motifs and striped waistband elevate the conventional look of this skirt. 

What We Don’t Love: It’s only available in one colorway.

When you’re heading directly from the court to brunch, you need a tennis skirt that is both stylish and functional. Luckily, this flirty and feminine option from Tory Burch looks more elegant than your traditional options, yet still provides professional level performance.

Of course, the sophisticated design details are what initially caught our eye: A dainty, laser-cut flower pattern covers the skirt, something we haven’t seen in any other items. The striped elastic waistband ties the entire style together by amping up the preppy factor. Don’t let the delicate features fool you, though — this was made for actually playing tennis.

Made with four-way stretch fabric, the skirt moves with you as you race to hit the ball. This material also wicks away sweat and odor, so you’ll still feel fresh while you recharge post-match. Hidden underneath the pleated skirt, you’ll find built-in boy shorts with a large pocket for storing extra tennis balls.

Truthfully, there’s not much we’d change about the skirt. We simply wish there were more color options available. Right now, it’s only offered in classic white.

Size: XS–XL | Material: Polyester, elastane | Colors: 1

Best Plus-Size

Spanx Get Moving Skort

Nordstrom

What We Love: The perforated side offers more wiggle room and provides easy access to the pocket.

What We Don’t Love: If you plan on wearing this while playing tennis, the pocket placement is rather annoying.

We can always count on Spanx to create size inclusive staples. In cooler temperatures, we’re all about the faux leather leggings, but as we get into the full swing of summer, we’re swapping them out for this fun and flirty skort. 

Under the flowy 14.25-inch skirt, you’ll find Spanx’s fan-favorite Booty Boost shorts that smooth and sculpt your bum, helping you to look and feel your best. Along the right side of the curve-hugging shorts is a handy stretch pocket that easily stores phones and credit cards. We also appreciate that you don’t have to hike up the skirt to reach it. Instead (and unlike the pockets on other options) it’s perforated like a curtain, creating a quick peek-a-boo moment and allowing for more space. 

However, this stretchy pocket is located on the right side of the garment, meaning that if you’re left handed, you might find this location rather challenging.

Size: XS–3XL | Material: Nylon, elastane, polyester | Colors: 3

Best with Pockets

Love Bonito cheak Courtside Skirt

Love Bonito

What We Love: This skirt strikes a perfect balance between fashionable and practical. 

What We Don’t Love: It only has one pocket.

Part of what makes tennis skirts so appealing are their flirty and feminine silhouettes, but looks will only get you so far on the court. “Most tennis skirts are just riding trends. This non-pleated option is both cute and functional,” says InStyle commerce writer, Bianca Kratky. “While I have plenty of tennis skirts, this is the only one I actually play tennis in. It has a side pocket that’s a necessity for storing tennis balls.”

Along with the utilitarian pocket, the front slit allows for quick movement, so you can freely dash after rogue balls. The skirt comes in basic white and black, and if you really want to feel like a champion, we suggest pairing it with the brand’s matching Polo Bra for a cohesive look.

Best on Amazon

ZUTY High-Waisted Tennis Skirt

Amazon

What We Love: There are four convenient pockets sewn into the skirt.

What We Don’t Love: This runs slightly big, so it’s best to size down.

We always rely on Amazon for affordable, trendy fashion. And when it comes to tennis skirts, few compare to the quality or design of this style. Coming in at 18-inches long, it's a more modest option than others, especially for anyone on the taller side or those who prefer not to show much skin. When it comes to pockets, this design has plenty: There are two stretchy compartments on the sides, one zippered pocket in the back, and a slim, hidden waistband pouch to throw your keys or cash.

Despite being made with stretchy fabric and an adjustable drawstring, this runs a bit big. For a comfortable, figure-skimming fit, we recommend sizing down.

Best Pleated

Wilson

What We Don’t Love: With exposed shorts in the back, this doesn’t have the typical tennis skirt look people may be after.

Yes, technically most tennis skirts have built-in shorts, but generally, the flowy overlay keeps them hidden. However, since this design from Wilson is a skort, you’ll find a traditional pleated mock-skirt in the front and shorts on full display in the back. One of the only downsides to a pleated skirt is when it gets wrinkled — the zigzag crinkles ensure the pleats never look as crisp and smooth as they did before. What to Keep in Mind

Material

All of the tennis skirts on our list are either made from polyester or nylon. Both materials are stretchy and comfortable, but if you plan on wearing the skirt on the court, you’ll want to check for technical qualities like breathability and quick-drying capabilities. The Athleta Advantage Skort offers both, plus add UVA and UVB protection. 

Keep in mind that polyester and spandex compositions differ between brands. One company’s material may feel thick and weighty, while another may be airy and lightweight. “It's definitely worth investing in a quality, thicker fabric,” says California-based personal stylist Sahra Schukraft Brandt. A heavier textile also tends to be more durable as well as opaque, so you won’t have any wardrobe malfunctions while out and about.

Style

The biggest question when it comes to tennis skirts or sporty skorts is whether you want a pleated or straight design. One style isn’t superior to the other; it’s all about your personal preference.

“I’m partial to the pleated styles because I feel less restricted and restrained when running and lunging,” says professional stylist Neelo Noory. 

Model Alexis Ren shares a similar sentiment, but likes to switch things up with straight styles, depending on her mood. “I’ll always be a sucker for a pleated skirt because it’s fun and flirty — but straight skirts have a classic feel and are easier to style.”

Of course, tennis skirt designs are constantly evolving. Take the Wilson Farrah Pleated Skort for example. It incorporates the traditional pleated element into an innovative open-back short design. Opt for a style that aligns with your personal aesthetic — there’s plenty to choose from.

Your Questions, Answered

How to style a tennis skirt for the daytime?

Professional stylist and founder of Wardrobe Whisperer, Jessica Cadmus, offers a great rule of thumb for styling tennis skirts during the day. “If at least one piece of clothing is made of fabric that you’d wear off the court, you can take the whole outfit off the court.”

For example, if you’re looking for a top where you can play tennis then go straight to brunch, Cadmus suggests a fabric that is neither athletic nor technical, like a knit. She usually goes for something fitted, so it “snatches the waist” and balances out the typical flouncy skirt silhouette.

How do you keep tennis whites white?

Many tennis clubs require players to don all-white outfits, so maintaining a pristine, stain-free skirt is a must. To keep the skirt impossibly fresh looking, Schukraft Brandt suggests immediately treating any discoloration or stain. “For crisp whites, my favorite cleaner is a mix of hydrogen peroxide and baking soda,” she explains. “For more cream or ecru whites, baking soda and vinegar or plain soda water do the trick.”
