Tributes pour in as Playboy model dies at just 38 years old

Playboy model Tabby Brown has tragically passed away at the age of 38-years-old, her manager has confirmed.

The model and actress was known for her TV appearances on the UK version of The Bachelor, as well as appearing on high profile ads, including Canon, Virgin Atlantic, AXE, and Lynx.

Tributes pour in for Playboy model Tabby Brown

Tabby’s cause of death has not yet been confirmed, however, friends, families, and celebrities have paid tributes. Condolences and messages have poured in on her Instagram to remember the star.

The 38-year-old had previously dated Manchester City soccer players, Raheem Sterling and Mario Balotelli.

She’d also featured in music videos for singers including Snoop Dogg, B.O.B, and Dizzee Rascal.

Her latest Instagram post is full of tributes, including from fitness model Louise Glover.

She wrote: “Omg Tabby Brown I’m [heartbroken emoji] for you! I’m going to miss you soo much. Always a true, loving, caring fun & beautiful friend… we always said we have to make the most of our lives x x rip beautiful x x”

“RIP Tabby, you will be truly missed. You were one of a kind,” wrote one friend.

Vas J Morgan, who appeared on The Only Way is Essex, wrote: “My heart is broken.”

“You may not be here anymore, but you’re still in our hearts,” wrote another.

“Wow. Just heard. Ready in peace you beautiful soul.”

Tabby opens up on life before modeling

Before being a successful model, the 38-year-old revealed “all she did was study” on an episode of Snoop Dogg’s YouTube series, Curren$y, Models & Blowfly, back in 2012.

After getting her Bachelor’s and Master’s, the model worked as a business manager, before getting a commercial for Virgin Atlantic.

Speaking about leaving the business for modeling, she said she could always come back to the business.

“I’m not going to be able to model when I’m 50, so why not?” she told the Young, Wild & Free rapper, who happened to disagree.
