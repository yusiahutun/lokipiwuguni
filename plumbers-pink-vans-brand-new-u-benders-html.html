Plumbers in pink vans re-brand with new name U-Benders

Plumbers in pink vans with name U-Benders prove a big hit with both gay and straight customers (including Julian Clary)

  • London-based firm adopted controversial name and painted vans pink
  • It claims customers are delighted and camp comic Julian Clary is a fan
  • But some have suggested that the use of homophobic slur is offensive

A plumbing company has caused a stir by naming itself 'U-Benders' and painting its vans pink.

The firm's distinctive vehicles are often seen driving around London, and the company claims it is popular with both gay and straight customers.

But some people are less amused by the U-benders pun, suggesting that it could be seen as 'really offensive' by gay people thanks to its jokey use of a homophobic slur.

Memorable: A plumbing company calling itself 'U-Benders' has deployed pink vans on the streets of London

Controversy: The firm claims that its customers love the name, but some people have suggested it could be construed as being offensive

U-Benders, based in Battersea, south-west London, insists it has received an enthusiastic response from customers since painting four vans pink and adopting its new branding three years ago.

It even hands out bright pink mugs blazoned with the company name to ensure that no one forgets it.

Among the firm's satisfied customers is Julian Clary, the gay comedian known for his flamboyant persona.

The humorous name was intended to be 'eyecatching and memorable' rather than controversial, according to a spokesman.

Gifts: The company hands out pink mugs with its name on to loyal customers

Memorabilia: Motorists without a pink van of their own can hang this U-Benders air freshener in their vehicle

'We're not trying to make a political statement,' he said. 'U-bends are used in almost everything we do.

'It doesn't matter to us whether our customers are straight, gay, bisexual or what have you - it only matters to us we can fix their pipes.

'Julian Clary loves us, we do all his plumbing for him.'

However, some people who have seen the company's vans on the streets are less amused by the outlandish branding.

Fan: Camp comedian Julian Clary is said to be an enthusiastic customer of U-Benders

'Not sure if this branding is really clever or really offensive,' Andrew Nattan tweeted. 'Can you really call your plumbers U-Benders?'

The company says it has never had any complaints about the name - but it has been targeted by prank callers, some of whom appear to be patrons of the pub opposite its headquarters.

'We've had a few drunk people phone up and really go to town on it,' a spokesman said. 'And teenagers phone up, giggling away.'

The company is a division of WPJ Heating, which also owns 10 white vans which are rather more low-key.
