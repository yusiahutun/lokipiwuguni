Stephen Colbert tells Eminem not to hide his face behind a hoodie in stunt

Stephen Colbert took over a Michigan public access TV show and interviewed rapper Eminem.

Colbert, 51, brought his brand of satire to the Only In Monroe show in a YouTube video posted on Wednesday.

'Since my last show ended in December, I’ve been itching to host a talk show again but my new theater’s not ready yet, so I decided to head over to Monroe, Michigan, look around, and give it a Michigander,' Colbert said at the start of the 41-minute video as he watered a fake plant. 

Cable show: Stephen Colbert took over a public access cable show in Michigan and interviewed Eminem in a YouTube video posted on Wednesday

Colbert wrapped up his Comedy Central show The Colbert Report in December and will start hosting The Late Show With Stephen Colbert in September.

His guest on Only In Monroe also included the show's regular hosts Michelle Baumann who is a registered nurse and former Miss America Kaye Lani Rafko-Wilson.

Colbert practiced the beauty pageant wave with them and also painted his nails. 

Special guest: Eminem was interviewed for about 20 minutes on the public access show

He later addressed a 'sensitive issue tearing Monroe apart' as he tried to mend the rift between Yelp reviewer Mark M. and Jerry's Frenchtown Bar & Grill.

Colbert presented an oversized check of $4.75 made out to Mark M. to make up for the contested bill.

Eminem appeared 22 minutes into the video and Colbert touched on several topics with the Detroit native. 

Fake plant: Stephen opened the show by watering a fake plant

Pageant wave: Regular hosts Michelle Baumann and Kaye Lani Rafko-Wilson worked with Stephen on his beauty pageant wave

Sensitive issue: Stephen addressed a Yelp review by Mark M. that has been tearing Monroe apart

Problem solver: The comedian presented a $4.75 check to Mark M. to make up for the contested bill

Colbert told the rapper that he should take his hoodie down.

'That's such a cute face. Why hide it under a hoodie? That's your little money maker,' Colbert said.

Eminem removed his hood only to have Colbert suggest putting it back on.

'My mistake,' he said. 

Hoodie up: Eminem wore a hoodie that Stephen suggested he take down

Colbert then asked Eminem if he was concerned about children being exposed to foul language in rap.

'I don't do any of that,' Eminem replied.

Colbert then asked him to give a message to other rappers about keeping it clean. 

Keeping it clean: The rapper at Stephen's urging other rappers to keep it clean

 'You don't have to do it. Watch your f***ing mouths,' Eminem said.

'Thank you. That was refreshing,' Colbert said.

They also talked about the upcoming boxing movie Southpaw in which Eminem has produced the soundtrack.

A clip for the movie starring Jake Gyllenhaal oddly featured two white cats sparring.

Southpaw clip: A clip for the movie Southpaw showed two cats fighting in a ring

Eminem sought to perform on the show but was told by Colbert they were running out of time.

He instead helped read upcoming events on the community calendar.

The interview ended with Colbert and Eminem waving a long good-bye for over a minute.

The Late Show With Stephen Colbert will air on September 8 on CBS. 

Long good-bye: Eminem and Stephen waved good-bye for more than a minute at the end of the show
