Kenya's Inflation: Cement and Steel Prices Double, Some Hardware Report Shortage

  • In the North Rift region, for instance, a 50kg bag of cement is retailing at KSh 1,000 in some stores up from KSh 550 a few weeks ago
  • Cement manufacturers cited the increased cost of raw materials and high taxation as the reasons for the skyrocketing prices
  • Steel prices have also shot up with D8 bars now retailing at KSh 780 up from KSh 450 in January while D10 bars are being sold at KSh 1,150 up from KSh 600

Kenyans investing in the real estate sector and those constructing their homes have been forced t dig deeper into their pockets after the prices of cement and steel doubled.

Cement manufacturers cited the increased cost of raw materials and high taxation as the reasons for the skyrocketing prices.

"We have been forced to increase our prices because of high taxation, the cost of electricity is high, the fuel price is high, and importation of raw materials like concrete admixtures has also risen," Daily Nation quoted a representative of the Kenya Federation of Master Builders.

New cement prices in Kenya

In the North Rift region, for instance, a 50kg bag of cement is retailing at KSh 1,000 in some stores up from KSh 550 a few weeks ago.

In Nairobi, the price for the same bag ranges from KSh 680 to KSh 750 depending on the brand.

Cement out of stock

Hardware in some parts of Kenya have gone out of stock with buyers forced to wait.

"We currently do not have cement in stock but we are expecting to get it in the afternoon. Kindly check with us around 2pm,' a seller in Kawangware said.

Steel prices

Steel prices have also shot up with D8 bars now retailing at KSh 780 up from KSh 450 in January.

Kenya milk shortage

In other news, there is an ongoing shortage of processed milk in different parts of the country that has seen the price of the commodity rise.

Nairobi, Nyandarua and Mombasa counties and parts of Nyanza and Western regions are among parts of the country experiencing the shortage.

Retailers say the shortage has worsened in the last two weeks, explaining the recent price rise.

Milk prices have risen by up to KSh 10 for a 500ml packet, to retail between KSh 60 and KSh 63.

Kenya Dairy Board (KDB) managing director Margaret Kibogy said that the expected rains would reverse the situation.
