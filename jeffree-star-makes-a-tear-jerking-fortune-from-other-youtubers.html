Jeffree Star makes an eye-watering fortune from other YouTubers

Jeffree Star’s beauty empire stands tall amidst all the controversies, with the business mogul’s earnings come from an array of investments including a shipment and fulfillment centre.

From MySpace fame to a full-fledged beauty empire that led to Jeffree Star’s incredible $200 million (approximate) net worth, the celebrity‘s rise to fame as one of the richest YouTubers in the world is inspiring. Along with the beauty guru’s Jeffree Star Cosmetics line, he expanded his business by investing in a number of other ventures.

Inside Jeffree Star’s crazy fortune and how he expanded it beyond the beauty realm

Jeffree Star started as a MySpace icon and released his studio album, Beauty Killer. But, soon enough, the digital content creator learned to expand his career beyond being an influencer. In 2014, Jeffree established his brand Jeffree Star Cosmetics.

In a candid chat with Shane Dawson, Jeffree revealed his need to reinvent himself as his previous music career made him ‘bankrupt.’ The artist made his comeback via YouTube, promoting his own beauty brand and reviewing other products.

Along with Jeffree making a fortune out of his YouTube fame and makeup products, the star reveals that he owns an entire shipment and fulfillment center, which facilitates shipping for a lot of brands. The 38-year-old business icon also owns a merchandise company.

Jeffree said: “I print and manufacture everything myself. So I have about 10 businesses that I’m currently running besides my brand, which is my cosmetics company.”

Jeffree Star’s real estate

Jeffree Star knows where the coin is. After forming his ultra-successful beauty business, which continues to be popular, Jeffree is trying his luck in real estate.

The celebrity bought a property in Hidden Hills worth $14.58 million in 2019 and sold it for $16.7 million, making a whopping profit of over $2 million.

After trying his luck in real estate, the YouTube creator has moved to his Star Yak Ranch which reportedly occupies more than 600 acres in Casper, Wyo., where he has a farm and more.
