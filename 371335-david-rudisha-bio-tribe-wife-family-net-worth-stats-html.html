David Rudisha bio: tribe, wife, family, net worth, stats

No country has produced as many excellent two-lap runners as Kenya has. In fact, if you look at a list of the fastest 800m runners in history, you will find that more than half of them are Kenyans. However, the one who stands out above all the rest is David Rudisha. He is not just the greatest 800m runner ever, but also one of the greatest track athletes.

David Rudisha entered the public limelight when he competed in the 2006 World Junior Athletics Championships in Beijing and emerged as the winner. Since that time, he has participated in numerous global athletics events, emerging the winner and breaking the world records.

Currently, he is the world record holder in the 800m race. He holds the three fastest, six of the eight fastest, and half of the twenty fastest times ever run in this event.

David Rudisha biography

David Rudisha was born on December 17, 1988, in Kilgoris, Rift Valley. He is the sixth born in a family of seven. The name of his father is Daniel Rudisha, while that of his mother is Naomi. David Rudisha's family boasts of a strong background in athletics.

His father was a former runner who won the silver medal during the 1968 Olympics as part of the Kenyan 4 × 400 m relay team, while his mother is a former 400 m hurdler.

Rudisha's father passed on a year ago, having battled diabetes and high blood pressure for most of his later years. For his high school education, he enrolled at Kimuron Secondary School in Iten.

Career

David debuted his career as a professional athlete in 2005. In April 2005, he was under the tutelage of Brother Brolm O'Connell, an Irish missionary and Geography teacher at St. Patrick's Iten school.

Japheth Kimutai was one of the trainees of Brother Colm, and he recommended Rudisha to James Templeton. He joined the group of athletes managed by Templeton. Some of the runners in this group included Kimutai, Augustine Choge, and Bernard Lagat.

He started his career as a 400m runner as he wanted to step in the shoes of his father and emulate him. However, his coach encouraged him to switch to 800m. The following year he clocked 1.49.6 in a two-lap event when running on a dirty track. This was an amazing performance for a 15-year-old running on a dirt track.

In November 2010, at the age of 21, he became the youngest ever athlete to win the IAAF World Athlete of the Year award. On August 22, 2010, he broke the World Record set by his predecessor Wilson Kipketer in the 800m race and set a new record during the ISTAF meeting in Berlin.

Is David Rudisha married?

Rudisha is a happily married man. The name of David Rudisha's wife is Lizzy Naanyu. Rudisha and Naanyu tied the knot in Kilgoris in 2010 in a Maasai wedding ceremony, soon after he was crowned IAAF World Athlete of the Year. The couple has been blessed with three children.

David Rudisha car accident

On August 24, 2019, Kenyans awoke to the sad news that Rudisha's car had collided with a bus head-on near Keroka, Kenya. David Rudisha's car was written off. He was rushed to a nearby hospital. David stated that he was fine and had not sustained any serious injuries.
