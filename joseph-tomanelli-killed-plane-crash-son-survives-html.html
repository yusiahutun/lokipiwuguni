Joseph Tomanelli killed in plane crash, son survives

A young man has been found alive among the wreckage of a plane crash that killed his father.

Daniel Tomanelli, 21, was hospitalized in the single-engine plane crash that killed his father, 56-year-old Joseph T. Tomanelli, on Monday evening in Wallingford, Connecticut. The son is expected to survive.

The aircraft appeared to be attempting to land when it crashed through a chain link fence, crossed a road, and struck a berm, police said. Investigators have not said who was piloting the plane when it crashed.

The crash was reported on Hanover Street, which runs parallel to the runways at Meriden-Markham Municipal Airport, at 6.28pm.

Joseph Tomanelli registered a 1971 Piper PA-28-180 in 2006, according to federal records.

Resident Fallon Wagner told the Record Journal that she saw the plane circling overhead, and then abruptly turn and fly back in the opposite direction.

'I actually said aloud to myself, "Something is not right, that plane is going to crash",' Wagner said. 

Wagner said she entered a store and came back out to see black smoke rising from the crash site. 

'I knew immediately what must have happened,' Wagner said. 'My heart breaks for the victims. So scary.'

A single-engine Piper similar to the one in the crash is shown in this file photo

Other witnesses reported hearing a 'boom' and seeing black smoke.

Fire crews arrived and extinguished the fire by 6.40pm. Daniel Tomanelli was found alive a few feet from the wreckage. It is not known whether he crawled out, was thrown clear or was pulled free. 

Daniel Tomanelli was transported to a hospital with a broken femur, and is recovering.

Joseph Tomanelli was a primary care physician from Cheshire with an office in Wallingford. 

'Dr. Tomanelli was a well-known and esteemed primary care physician in our community for years and will be tremendously missed,' Hartford HealthCare said in a statement. 'Our thoughts and prayers are with all who knew him.'

The National Transportation Safety Board and Federal Aviation Administration are investigating the crash.

It is the fifth small plane crash in Connecticut in the past 12 months.
