15 best black metal bands that every rock fan should listen to

Black metal is a specific subgenre of heavy metal music that has soldiered on since the 1960s. Although old songs have lost their sheen and many bands have kept inventing new subgenres, black metal has remained steadfast. Many black metal songs are not complex or technique-heavy. Instead, the best black metal bands excel at creating an atmosphere that immerses a listener into a dark sound escape that elicits a specific reaction. It can put you in a sad and reflective state or engage you in an energetic and intense one.

Over the years, black metal bands have conquered the arrangements of festivals across the globe. Furthermore, they have been passionate whenever they have delivered their music. Whether that delivery is melancholic or aggressive, it hits the listener with that passion and feeling with no fear of judgement. However, all that cannot happen if a band is not creative in creating fascinating rhythms for the vocals demanded in the subgenre.

15 best black metal bands

Black metal has proven hard to eliminate. While sensational violence and a flirtation with the occult have often threatened to eclipse the genre's artistic merits, it is ultimately the music that has given this dark practice its longevity. Here are some of the best black metal bands that have made the genre a must-listen for rock fans.

1. Bathory

Bathory is a black metal band often considered the greatest in Sweden. The band pioneered black metal and Viking metal in Sweden and became the blueprint of the subgenre in the entire Scandinavian. Although the group dissolved in the early 2000s, they remain some of the best writers of the industry's most evil records ever produced.

Furthermore, their songwriting skills have passed the test of time. For example, in 1987, they wrote the song Under the Sign of the Black Mark. The song was a masterpiece and has since become one of the most copied sounds ever within the realm of black metal. In addition, Bathory released many albums that operated in a dark and hellish atmosphere, a factor that solidified them as one of the greatest black metal bands of all time.

2. Burzum

Burzum was a Norwegian black metal music project established in 1991 by Varg Vikernes. The group was one of the best Norwegian black metal bands because they played a significant role in the rise of black metal during the second wave. Varg Vikernes mainly was alone when he recorded all the Burzum albums.

Although the band never went live throughout its lifespan, its songs became fan-favourites because the lyrics were dark and filled with fantasy and mythology. Furthermore, Burzum ensured his songs had an excellent combination of ambience and metal, two factors that made them masterpieces. Even today, fans of the genre consider Burzum the black metal Mozart because of the high skill level in composing its pieces.

3. Celtic Frost

Celtic Frost was a black metal band from Zurich formed in 1984. Although Celtic Frost was a black metal band, they were also best known for their creativeness in developing extreme metal and avant-garde metal. The group released its debut album in 1984 titled Morbid Tales. Morbid Tales had a thrash metal intensity that influenced death metal.

Celtic Frost had a specific form of music that combined elements of different extreme metal styles. Most of their albums are akin to gothic and doom metal. 4. Darkthrone

Darkthrone first came to existence in 1986 as a death metal band. However, by the end of 1990, the group changed their singing and composing songs and embraced a black metal style. The reason for choosing black metal is because they got their inspiration from other leading bands, such as Bathory and Celtic Frost.

Unlike many black metal bands of the 1990s, Darkthrone is the only Norwegian band that continues to produce songs even in 2021. Although they may not be the wealthiest black metal band, the group has continued to evolve their artistic vision and sounds in all the albums it has released. And for 35 years since the band formed, it has remained the most subtle, iconic, and musically refined pioneer of the second wave of black metal.

5. Dimmu Borgir

Dimmu Borgir is one of the most popular black metal bands from Norway, formed in 1993. The band was first inspired by the incarnations of the black metal genre from the 80s and early 90s. They made their debut into the underground metal scene by rehearsing unofficial cassette tapes.

Soon afterwards, Dimmu Borgir mastered the art of composing black metal songs. The group began producing resplendent music in melodic vocals, sombre guitar works, haunting keyboard melodies, and destructive drums.

In 1995, they released their first album titled For all Lid that offered a somewhat splendid view of heavy metal that came off as more ambitious. As of 2021, the band has released ten studio albums, two compilation albums, three video albums, 15 music videos, four extended plays, and six singles.

6. Emperor

Emperor is a top black metal band from Norway formed in 1991. Many people consider it the most ambitious black metal band ever to exist. Initially, the group consisted of three members, Ihsahn, Samoth, and Trym.

7. Enslaved

Enslaved is an extreme metal and black metal band from Haugesund, Norway, formed in June 1991. Initially, the group began as a traditional metal band. But over the years, they reinvented their music and settled in a more progressive sound. In addition, changing their music styles made it easier for people to listen to their songs.

Enslaved released their debut album in 1994 titled Vikingligr Veldi. Although the album was not a commercial success, it stood the test of time and remained crucial to the black metal genre. The band released 15 studio albums, two video albums, six music videos, and three extended plays throughout its three decades in music.

8. Gorgoroth

Gorgoroth is one of the most famous black metal bands from Bergen, Norway. The group came into existence in 1992 when guitarist Infernus formed it. The group remains popular in Norway because of their satanic theme, a factor they have incorporated even in their concerts. Furthermore, they have produced anti-Christian lyrics that have remained controversial even today.

Gorgoroth's best songs include A Sorcery Written in Blood, Revelation of Doom, and Litani til Satan. Gorgoroth produced six studio albums, three live albums, and one video album. Although they mixed their styles, they remained consistent in making their songs more abrasive and purely satanic.

9. Hate Forest

While many black metal bands find their origin in Scandinavian nations, Hate Forest is an exception. It is a black metal band from Ukraine formed by Roman Saenko in 1995. Even though it is a Ukrainian band, it drew its inspiration from Scandinavian black metal bands. Hate Forest began as a death metal band and later changed their musical styles to incorporate other heavy metal subgenres.

10. Hellhammer

Hellhammer did not have a long lifespan in the music industry. The group came together in 1982 and dissolved in 1984. But despite singing and composing songs for only two years, Hellhammer became a pivotal band in influencing black metal in Switzerland. After their disbandment, the members founded a second black metal group called Celtic Frost.

Hellhammer only released one commercial song titled Apocalyptic Raids. Although they had several singles and compilation albums, they released the songs years later. But nobody can deny Hellhammer’s role in the rise of extreme metal. Even without realising it, they created a legacy that endures even today. The late release of the songs and excellent reception from fans adequately documents that legacy.

11. Immortal

Immortal is a black metal band founded in 1991 and based in Bergen, Norway. The group’s founding members were guitarists and worked with several drummers over the years. Immortal’s music was death metal influenced by other acts, such as Possessed and Morbid Angel. They then changed their music style to black metal, primarily because of the influence of Bathory and Celtic Frost.

In 1991, Immortal released a self-titled extended play that incorporated black metal lyrics. They followed it up with a debut album titled Diabolical Fullmoon Mysticism, released in 1992. Although the band had many musical styles, it mainly focused on heroic, Nordic, the fusion of gothic and demon-and-battle-filled themes.

12. Mayhem

Mayhem is a black metal band formed in Oslo, Norway, in 1984. The band was one of the many founders of the Norwegian black metal scene, and many people consider them the pioneers of the genre in Norway. However, unlike many music bands, Mayhem enjoyed a loyal following immediately after its first demo and extended play.

13. Mercyful Fate

Mercyful Fate is a heavy metal and black metal band from Copenhagen, Denmark. It came into existence in 1981 after vocalist King Diamond, and guitarist Hank Shermann formed it. Instantly, it became a success and influenced the first wave of black metal in Denmark in the early 1980s.

Since 1981, Mercyful Fate has released seven studio albums, three compilation albums, six music videos, three singles, and two extended plays. Their songs helped establish the style upon which black metal artists of today have built their careers. Some of their best songs include A Dangerous Meeting, The Uninvited Guest, and Dead Again.

14. Satyricon

Satyricon is a black metal band formed in 1991 and based in Oslo, Norway. The band released its first album in 1994, titled Dark Medieval Times, despite taking one year to produce it. Although the album featured a Middle Ages theme, it had many components of black metal blast beats.

Despite starting as a black metal band, Satyricon changed its music style from the mid-2000s. The band’s most recent albums have abandoned the black metal style and focused on old school rock, heavy metal, and post-rock music. After releasing nine studio albums, Satyricon’s most successful album came out in 2013. The self-titled album topped the charts in Norway and other Scandinavian countries.

15. Venom

You cannot complete a list of black metal bands without mentioning Venom. Almost any discussion about black metal begins with referencing the band. In the black metal industry, Venom gets the credit of starting the genre in a way. The band first came into existence in 1978 when a new wave of British heavy metal was ending.
