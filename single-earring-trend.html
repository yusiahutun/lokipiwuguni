How to style the single earring in 2024

The single earring trend is nothing new. Ever since stacking became the jewellery styling norm circa ten years ago, we have leaned much more towards single earrings that we can buy solo. Individual earrings enable creative flair – they make it fun and fuss-free to fill an entire ear with personalised studs, huggies, and hoops. This season, however, the single earring has been souped up – major maisons and fine jewellery brands are celebrating the single statement earring with some seriously supersized styles.

The spring/summer runways led the charge – at Fendi, models sported one long, sleek golden bar that extended from the ear all the way down the neck, echoing the gleaming gold anklets that secured the girls’ pumps and sandals. Other models wore oversized ear climbers in mixed-metal tones, occasionally contrasted with a bite-sized stud on the other side to enhance asymmetry. At Givenchy, last season’s love of chunky, silver Y2K-inspired jewels continued in earnest – chubby hoop earrings scintillated with large pearl orbs, sometimes stacked two at a time on just one ear.

Loewe showcased some of the most opulent single earrings for SS24 – organic shapes embraced the ear like bejewelled sculptures, each set with pink, turquoise and white crystals, while Balmain wrapped one ear in sparkling cuffs, effortlessly illuminating cheek bones. Not all the single earring looks were quite so ornate; at Dior, simple gold hoops were worn on just one side, and at Gucci a chic jumbo ‘G’ motif swung from a single earlobe.

Dior, Balmain, Givenchy

If you’ve played it safe with paired earrings all your life, it can be tricky to know where to start when it comes to single earring curation. Designers such as Sophie Bille Brahe, Jessica McCormack and Anita Ko offer petite yet punchy pieces that offer the perfect starting point, while Messika and Éera create cool and contemporary designs dusted with diamonds. For those looking for something a little bolder, look to major maisons such as Saint Laurent and Prada – their monograms and iconic motifs make instant impact.

Gucci and Loewe

If you’re keen to avoid the almost inevitable question from those slightly less clued up on earring trends – “have you lost an earring?” – then choose a statement style that comes with an asymmetrical stud for your other ear, such as Boucheron’s ‘Serpent Bohème Solarité’ design, which dazzles in 18-karat gold and diamonds. For piercing veterans, it can look unsightly to leave lots of holes empty, so be conscious of space and consider trying barely-there studs to create a constellation effect up the ear that will quietly complement a standout piece in your lowest piercing. Whichever style you choose, adorn yourself on the double.
