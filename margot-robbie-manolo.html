Margot Robbie has been wearing Manolos during the Barbie press tour

The press tour for Barbie has been nothing short of sensational with Margot Robbie and her new stylist Andrew Mukamal pulling out all the stops, referencing specific Barbie outfits from years gone by, and delighting fans in the process. Although the tour was forced to pause while the strikes took place, the actress has been at multiple Barbie screenings and events this week, and the pink wardrobe has been back out in full force.

Robbie has worn a number of big brands during the tour, everything from custom Schiaparelli and vintage Versace to Balmain, Bottega and Valentino, but she seems to have chosen a favourite designer when it comes to her footwear.

Following in the footsteps of Carrie Bradshaw, who always favoured the designer's heels, Robbie has turned to Manolo Blahnik for almost every major stop on the Barbie press tour.

Albert L. Ortega//Getty Images

The Spanish designer has custom designed most of the looks that she's accessorised her ensembles with, creating Barbie-fied versions of his most beloved styles – and it's a task that Blahnik revelled in.

"My shoes are elegant, fun but are also incredibly comfortable, ideal for red carpet moments," he told us. "Barbie is truly iconic, so when asked to provide shoes for the wonderful Margot Robbie, whom I adore, I wanted to update some of my most successful designs that have been in my collections for decades, with a Barbie twist of course. Pink is one of my absolute favourite colours; from cerise to powder pink, I love them all – and so it has been a joy to reimagine these styles in a host of pink hues."

Blahnik gave a Barbie makeover to his beloved Irene pumps, a pointed court shoe, which he created in white with a hot pink toe cap and a hot pink heel. Robbie used these to accessorise her Versace suit, which she wore to the Seoul premiere, and was based on a 1985 Barbie called 'Day to Night'.

Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

Another perfect accessory came in the form of a bright pink iteration of Blahnik's Jada style, a beautiful mule which Robbie has worn on several occasions already, including when her and Mukamal recreated the 1964 'Sparkling Pink' Barbie moment with an embellished Moschino co-ord. Blahnik also created the Jada in patent black for the Los Angeles premiere where Robbie wore a 'Solo in the Spotlight'-inspired look from Schiaparelli.

Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

Most recently, Robbie wore another custom pair of Jada heels – this time in red – for a screening, to go with her 'Cherry Pie Picnic' Barbie outfit from Bottega. She also wore the pink style again with her feather-trimmed Sleeper pyjamas.

Eric Charbonneau//Getty Images

"Barbie has always remained modern and has developed over the decades to speak to each new generation," Blahnik says of his joy about being involved in the pro. "That’s what it takes to be iconic, relevance and adapting with the times."
