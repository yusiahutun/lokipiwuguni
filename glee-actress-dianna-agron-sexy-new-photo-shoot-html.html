Glee actress Dianna Agron in a sexy new photo shoot

The pom poms are long gone! Glee actress Dianna Agron swaps her cheerleader uniform for leather and lacy lingerie in a sexy new photo shoot

Best known as a clean-cut but catty cheerleader on Glee, Dianna Agron is ready to show off her sultry side.

The 27-year-old star of the Fox musical stripped down to silk stockings and a red satin slip for a provocative new photo shoot for Galore magazine.

Whether writhing on a blue fluffy rug or reclining back in a chair with just a purple fur jacket covering her lacy lingerie, the former teen star is embracing her adult transformation.

Prowling: Dianna Agron amped up the sex appeal for a sultry magazine shoot that was captured by photographer Ellen Von Unwerth

The Savannah, Georgia born-actress transformed from a Southern belle into a ravishing femme fatale for the raunchy shoot , that was captured for Galore by photographer Ellen Von Unwerth.

In the main images, she slips into a sparkly red negligee with blood red lips and dark eyes, as she stretches out clutching a leopard print blanket with a goblet of wine spilled beside her.

Diamonds drip from her wrist but the scene is meant to be anything but classy.

Catty lady: With cat's eye glasses and a purple fur coat, the Glee actress was transformed into a retro femme fatale

Blonde bombshell: The 27-year-old actress embraced her inner drama queen as she collapsed in a sea of diamonds, glitter and fur

She then changes into a far less damaged and more dynamic pose, in silk stockings and a black lace bra, with only the purple fur covering her torso, and garish gold cat's eye glasses on her face.

Dianna captures a more sultry, innocent persona in a later photo, as she allows a brown floral dress to slip off her arm while delicately biting her nails.

The groundbreaking collection also includes some artistic 1960s style black and white photos, with Argon in a skintight corset all-in-one and black high heels.

Big hair: Dianna's blonde mid-length locks were teased into a huge bouffant

Film noir: The black and white shot had a classic Hollywood feel with a dark twist

Walking through a desert garden filled with spiky cactus, she could have been captured mid-scene shooting a classic movie.

A final photo in a sheer polka dot dress seemingly captures a fun, fancy free side of Dianna's alter ego, but shredded tights and suggest that there is more to the story than meets the eye.

In the accompanying interview, the actress, who recently starred in The Family opposite Robert De Niro and Michelle Pfeiffer, told the magazine that she is sometimes tempted to get violent with a tennis racket like her character did in the drama.

Blurred lines: The shaky camera work added to the dramatic atmosphere of the shoot

Polka dots: This photo is the collection looks more light-hearted until you notice Argon's shredded tights

'There are definitely moments where you’d love to beat the s**t out of someone. Sure,' she confessed.

'People can be cruel, take advantage of young girls, you name it. But, my best tennis racket is the ability to use my words.

'Just have to be confident and remember to use them. Standing up for yourself, and putting someone in their place with wit is a truly admirable skill,' she advised.

Nearing the end of a troubled year, which included the death of co-star Cory Monteith, the actress better known as Quinn Fabray, is looking forward to a fresh start.

Breakout role: Dianna is best known as cheerleader and Glee club spy, Quin Fabray in the Fox musical

'2014 will be a year for new projects, new travels, and much happiness. I’ve had some hard things to get through, emotional loss in 2013, and through sadness comes deeper understanding and pursuit of happiness,' she told Galore.

'At least that’s what I think. Boring answer, a bit. But true. Maybe 2014 will be the year I also try something I am terrified of. Like scuba diving. Probably not, but maybe. Never say never.'

Dianna will next be seen in the film, Conspiracy on Jekyll Island, where she plays the role of Amelia in the financial thriller that also stars Minnie Driver.

'She’s a badass, she knows it… there is nothing exceptionally feminine about her and she is  unapologetic,' Argon said of her character. 'It’s delicious. It’s day two of me being here, we had a cast dinner last night and also had some vodka shots.'
