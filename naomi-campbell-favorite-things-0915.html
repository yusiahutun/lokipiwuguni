Naomi Campbell Shares Her Favorite TV Shows, Movies and More

Every product on this page was chosen by a Harper's BAZAAR editor. We may earn commission on some of the items you choose to buy.

1

TV Picks

Media Platforms Design Team

"I download TV shows and watch them on my laptop when I fly, or before I sleep. Right now I'm watching Netflix's Grace and Frankie, and Transparent, which is absolutely brilliant. And of course I watch Scandal. I'm a big Rupaul fan, so I always watch that. And then there's How To Get Away With Murder, Sons of Anarchy, American Horror Story, True Detective, Devious Maids, and Aquarius."

2

Jet, Set, Go

Media Platforms Design Team

"I love Brazil, I love Egypt, Jordan, Italy, Japan, Cambodia, Burma—all those types of places. I love to travel for pleasure. Even though I travel so much for work, nothing makes me happier than traveling for pleasure. I never travel without a Zohar, which is a Jewish religious text. "

3

Favorite Food

Media Platforms Design Team

"I love the Cipriani and Nobu restaurants all over the world. I always eat clean, healthy, Kosher food, and I start every day with green juice and apple cider, which have cleansing properties. I am a big believer in keeping the colon clean. I used to love chocolate, until I found out I was allergic and I can't eat it anymore. Instead, now I eat walnuts and raisins and fresh fruit, which release sugars more slowly into your body."

Nobu New York, noburestaurants.com; Cipriani restaurants, cipriani.com.

Good Luck Charms

Media Platforms Design Team

"I couldn't even start to tell you how many crystals I have. I keep a black tourmaline and a rose quartz in my bag, always. They signify protection and love, which is nice to have with you when you're traveling around the world every two seconds—not that I'm complaining! And then I bring others in my luggage."

5

Favorite Films

Media Platforms Design Team

"One of my favorites is As Good As It Gets, with Jack Nicholson. I also loved American Sniper. It was amazing to understand what men go through when they go to war to fight for their country, and when they come back—just their state of being. Steve McQueen's Twelve Years a Slave is also great, and The Color Purple."

On My Bedside Table
