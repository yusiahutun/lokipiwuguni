The ultimate guide to styling coloured gemstones

Dopamine dressing is one of the biggest trends this year and coloured gemstones are a sophisticated way to get in on the look. Emerald green and ruby red pieces were all over the recent catwalks, with top jewellery designers such as Sandy Leong, Fehmida Lakhany and Fabergé featuring the actual gemstones in their collections.

“Colours are inherently playful and coloured gemstones can be a source of fun as well,” says Gemfields gemmologist, Elena Basaglia. “Whether you combine them or wear them with diamonds, they complement each other. Anything goes!”

To help you shine bright in coloured gemstones, Harper’s Bazaar fashion editor Tilly Wheating offers her top tips for styling rubies and emeralds.

Should I choose gemstones that match my colouring?

“Look for gemstones that you love and will enjoy wearing. If you have green eyes, emeralds will enhance their colour but don’t get stuck on what works with your hair or eyes; a bright gem will enhance any look.”

Pro tip: People often gravitate to gemstone colours that make them feel good; red rouses passion and desire, and green is associated with spring and renewal.

How can I elevate a casual look with rubies and emeralds?

“I love pairing a white shirt and jeans with a ruby necklace or emerald drop earrings. It’s unexpectedly playful and elevates a casual look to something totally luxurious.”

Getty Images

Dress up a low-key look, such as a classic white shirt and jeans, with ruby jewellery, such as this Cage Ring and Hinge Choker by Sandy Leong and Teardrop Earrings by Fabergé

Great options include a gold choker necklace with a row of rubies or a diamond collar with an emerald drop. Emerald or ruby earrings will also add glamour to the everyday.

Can I combine different coloured gemstones?

“Don’t be shy about mixing coloured gemstones. New styles show rubies paired with turquoise and emeralds set alongside coral. That contrast of colour highlights the richness of the gemstones.”

Getty Images

Have fun with your gemstones. These earrings by Bina Goenka show how mixing colours can work so well

That being said, a monotone look is also a powerful statement. If you are in head-to-toe black or camel clothing, consider choosing just emeralds or rubies for a sophisticated feel.

How can I make coloured gemstones edgy?

“Mismatched earrings add edginess. Wear different shapes and sizes, such as a long ruby earring in one ear and a ruby stud in the other.”

And try layering colourful bracelets or necklaces. Start with one large anchor piece, such as a bold cuff or pendant necklace, and add more necklaces or bracelets around it.

What’s the best way to dress up with coloured gemstones?

“The little black dress is the perfect backdrop for a sparkling collar or a long necklace with ruby or emerald beads. Don’t be afraid to mix clashing colours: wear a green dress with rubies or a red dress with emeralds.”

//Getty Images

Emeralds, such as these Fabergé Necklace or Foliage Earrings, House of Meraki Evil Eye bracelet or GFG Artisia Ring, look striking when styled with a red dress

If you are wearing a backless gown, a long, colourful necklace draped down a bare back is stunning. Don’t forget statement earrings or ear crawlers set with bright gemstones.

What makes a good investment piece?

“I try to find the most timeless piece, one that I know I will wear for many occasions and can style with various looks.”

A gemstone ring is a wise investment because it becomes your signature jewel. Purchase a jewellery design that you can live in – an emerald line necklace works with everything and ruby earrings will bring glamour to any outfit.
