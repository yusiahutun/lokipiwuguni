Top 15 richest wrestlers of all time and their net worth

Wrestling is one of the most popular sports. Many people grew up watching wrestling matches and admiring the well-built bodies of wrestlers. While a lot of focus on wrestling has been on the entertainment side, many forget that it is one of the world's best-paying sports. So who are the richest wrestlers of all time?

Like any other sport, wrestling has evolved with time to be what it is today. The arenas, sponsorship deals, match categories, number of wrestlers, name it. Every facet of the sport has had a face-lift in the last couple of years. This includes transforming the lives of wrestlers from ordinary people to celebrities worth millions of dollars. Through wrestling, most of these fighters have been able to venture into other business investments, such as acting and selling merchandise.

1. Vince McMahon - $2.2 billion

Although most famous for his comical antics in and out of the ring, it is not surprising to learn that he was a professional wrestler long ago. Mr McMahon used his wrestling status to create his brand and move into several industries.

Currently, he is the CEO and majority shareholder of WWE. He has a Connecticut mansion worth $40 million and a Manhattan penthouse worth $12 million, among other properties.

2. The Rock - $320 million

The Rock is a former professional wrestler who used the platform to launch a thriving acting career. His last wrestling match was in 2016, which was the year Forbes ranked him as the highest-paid actor.

In 2017, he was the second-highest-paid actor. In 2019, he was also the highest-paid actor after earning $89.4 million before tax from his movie. Having ditched wrestling, he is now a full-time actor and producer but remains one of the richest wrestlers of all time.

3. Triple H - $150 million

Triple H, whose birth name is Paul Michael Levesque, has a net worth of $150 million. He is also the son-in-law of the wealthiest wrestler of all time, Vince McMahon. Triple H has been more of an executive in recent years than a wrestler, but he still gets paid for both.

In 2017, he earned $1.5 million for being a WWE Superstar and a further $650,000 for the vice president of talent executive role. He is also the founder and senior producer of NXT, which is a WWE developmental brand. With that success, there is no way Triple H can miss from the richest wrestlers 2020 list.

4. John Cena - $60 million

One of the most popular and equally successful wrestlers in recent years is John Felix Anthony Cena Jr alias, John Cena. The wrestler who has also diversified into acting has a net worth of about $60 million.

The Big Show is not just a giant of a man but also has a huge bank account. His career has earned him the $20 million net worth he has today. He is not young anymore, and thus his WWE appearances now are more of novelty acts than as a wrestler.

10. Chris Jericho - $18 million

Like most wrestling stars, Chris Jericho has also diversified his income streams. However, instead of venturing into an acting career, Jericho surprised many by launching a music career. He is the lead singer of the heavy metal band called Fozzy.

Currently, he is a part-time wrestler and a full-time singer. He makes wrestling appearances between his music concert tours. Apart from being a wrestler, singer, and writer, Jericho has also had many other jobs, including hosting several TV shows and producing a comedy web series based on his life in which he stars as himself.

11. Mick Foley - $18 million

Michael Francis Foley’s wrestling career lasted from 1983 to 2012 when he retired. His was the kind of match that used to be extreme and hardcore and could result in injuries on some occasions.

He took on a surprise career move after leaving wrestling when he transitioned into being multiple best-selling authors. He also performs in comedy clubs for his 20 Years of Hell tour in which he recalls the infamous Hell in a Cell match with The Undertaker.

12. The Undertaker - $17 million

The Undertaker had his first professional wrestling match in 1987, and he is yet to retire in 2020. He is one of the most famous and most gimmicked WWE superstars in the world. Despite having one of the longest wrestling careers, The Undertaker is nowhere near the top 10 in terms of finances. In the last decade or so, he has been much of a part-time wrestler, explaining why he has earned so little.

13. Shawn Michaels - $17 million

Michael Shawn Hickenbottom ties with The Undertaker with a net worth of $17 million. The Chandler native, known for his signature move, the Sweet Chin Music, has been a WWE superstar for 30 years.

At one point, he was the WWE poster-boy and won the world championship four times. In the last 18 years of his career, he was more of an on-camera personality and off-camera ambassador for WWE. Since his retirement in 2010, he has not stepped back into the ring.

14. Dave Bautista - $16 million

He only debuted in WWE at the age of 33 years, but that did not stop him from becoming a top superstar. He was a wrestler for nine years but also took advantage of the platform to become an actor.

He earned more from his roles in Guardians of The Galaxy and Avengers series than he made his entire wrestling career. Motivated by the top dollar he earns as an actor, Bautista has resisted going back into the ring as an actor.

15. Jeff Jarrett - $15 million

Jeff Jarrett joined the WWE circles in 1986. Due to his contribution, he got inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2018. He is also the co-founder of North America's second-best professional wrestling company, which was initially called Total Nonstop Action Wrestling before the name was changed to Impact Wrestling. After WWE CEO Vince McMahon fired Jarrett, he co-founded Impact Wrestling with his father.
