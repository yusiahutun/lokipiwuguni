Nairobi: 11-Year-Old Child Model Holds Charity Event to Feed, Clothe and Groom Street Families

  • Multiple award-winning child model Scarlet Nyawira recently held an event to uplift street families in Nairobi
  • The 11-year-old Grade 6 student offered food, clothes, shaves and massages to the forgotten families
  • She also used the gathering to educate them about the dangers of substance abuse and its effects

In a heartwarming display of compassion and community spirit, 11-year-old Scarlet Nyawira, aka Scarlet the Brand, has shown that age is no barrier to making a difference.

The model and dancer from Yari School in Ngara recently organized a charity event at Kiboro Primary School in Eastleigh to mark the International Day of Street Families.

Famous TikTokers graced Scarlet's event

Scarlet's mother, Catherine Karanja, told TUKO.co.ke that the event was a collaboration of 10 different foundations to provide food and engage with street families.

Her initiative aimed to provide immediate assistance and sought to empower and educate those in need.

It also educated them about the dangers of substance abuse and its effects.

As part of the event, there was a talent search, with the Grade 6 student bringing in famous TikTokers, including Oletile "The Dancing Maasai," Prince Chak, and Kelsin Lynn, to teach dance.

Street families enjoyed free shaves, massages

The talent search winners were awarded prizes, adding a touch of joy and celebration to the occasion.

Additionally, attendees had the opportunity to receive free shaves and massages from the professionals at Shave Cave Barbers.

"The idea was to enhance the sense of care and attention given to the street families who are mostly neglected by the public," Catherine said.

The proud mother disclosed that her daughter, an environmental activist, hopes to become a neurosurgeon in adulthood.

Scarlet is a multiple award-winning model

Scarlet holds several titles, the most recent being Kid Model of the Year 2022-2023, given at the Jamhuri Kenya Awards.

She was also Little Miss Independent Kenya 2nd Runners Up 2022, Little Miss Independent Kenya 2nd Runners Up, and Little Miss Confidence Kenya.

Despite her young age, the awards have brought her significant responsibilities, including launching a foundation in April 2023 to assist underprivileged children.

Through this foundation, Scarlet provides opportunities for children from various slums to showcase their talents.
