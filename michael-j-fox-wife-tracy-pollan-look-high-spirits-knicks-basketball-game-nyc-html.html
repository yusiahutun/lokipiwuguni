Michael J. Fox and wifeTracy Pollan look in high spirits at a Knicks basketball game in NYC

Michael J. Fox and his wife Tracy Pollan looked in high spirits at New York Knicks basketball game on Sunday.

The Back to the Future actor, 61, and the Family Ties actress, 62, sat court-side as they cheered on the NYC team, as they have been doing for many years.

Despite his ongoing battle with Parkinson's disease, Michael seemed to be enjoying the game and was even dressed in a Knicks bomber jacket.

He also had on a white t-shirt underneath his jacket, a pair of black jeans, and white Nike sneakers.

The Family Ties alum, which is the show he met Tracy on, wore his brown hair slightly unkept and threw on a pair of glasses while watching the game.

Day out: Michael J. Fox and his wife Tracy Pollan looked in high spirits at New York Knicks basketball game

Beside him, Tracy looked comfortable in a colorful knit sweater and a pair of wide-legged jeans, which she rolled up at the bottom.

In her lap, she rested her black leather jacket, and on her feet, she wore a pair of matching black boots.

The beauty accessorized with several pieces of gold jewelry, including necklaces and earrings.

She wore her blonde hair down in light waves and went for a minimal makeup look with only mascara, eyeliner, and some lipgloss.

The couple posed for a few photos during the game and were also captured in the midst of being enthralled in the game.

For one shot, the Teen Wolf star smiled sheepishly while his wife looked up at the jumbotron. In another one, she smiled at the camera as he looked at her.

The parents-of-four had a jam-packed weekend and also attended a Bruce Springsteen concert at Madison Square Garden.

Tracy shared several pictures and videos from the night to her Instagram stories for her 126K followers to see.

Throwback: He rose to fame in the '80s and became a household name due to his starring role in the Back to the Future franchise 

Michael and Tracy met after she was cast in 1985 as his girlfriend on his hit '80s sitcom Family ties, though the two didn't become a couple until they made the 1988 film Bright Lights, Big City.

Fox then discovered he had Parkinson's Disease after waking up from a night of drinking to find his pinky twitching in 1990. 

After it wouldn't stop, he went to a neurologist in 1991 and was diagnosed with the disease. He would go on to hide it for seven years by carrying props in his left hand.

Since his diagnosis, the iconic actor has focused his life on raising money to research the disease.

He has since raised over $1 billion in research funding via the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's Research.

In November, Fox was presented with an honorary Academy Award for his work surrounding the disease.

He also created a documentary called Still: A Michael J. Fox Movie, which debuted at Sundance in January and will go to Apple TV+ on May 12. 

In the film, fans will watch the actor, who has a net worth of $65M, working with a trainer to help build strength, walking through the streets of Manhattan, and repeatedly falling.

Deadline's Pete Hammond wrote that the documentary has 'the spirit of the kind of 80’s movies that helped make Fox a very big star on both big and small screens.' 

According to the awards columnist, the film explores Michael's life, from growing up in Canada to leaving school at 17 to gamble on a career in Hollywood.  

Despite the hardships of the disease, the actor said at the Sundance Q&A: 'It's an amazing life and I'm enjoying it.

'I love my family, I love what I do, I love that people react to what I do. I know that I can be an example of other people and help them deal with their issues without them asking me without me [to] put force of myself on them.'
