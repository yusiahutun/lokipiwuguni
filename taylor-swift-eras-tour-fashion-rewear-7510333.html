How to Rewear Your Taylor Swift Eras Tour Clothing IRL

So you managed to beg, borrow, or steal your way to one of the shows on Taylor Swift's Eras tour. Congratulations on living every Swiftie's dream! (We're not jealous or anything.) Now, it's time to figure out how to rewear the Eras tour costume you undoubtedly poured your heart and soul into putting together.

Swift's latest tour is tailor-made for fans who feel connected to each of her albums, from the ballgowns of Fearless to the vampy punk-adjacent snake motifs of Reputation. Whether you made the whole place shimmer in a beaded fringe bodysuit or stepped out in Lover's pastel palette, you probably want to keep wearing your Eras tour 'fit — for the mems, of course — but may not want to look like you're headed to an unofficial encore. That's where we come in, with 10 ways to keep wearing your Eras Tour clothes, long after the high of the three-and-a-half-hour concert has ended.

Midnights Era Shimmer Top or Dress

If you went all out and set your aesthetic to "Shimmer," à la Swift's Midnights era, you can easily rewear any crystal- or beaded fringe pieces you bought for the concert. To make even the most opulent sparkling fringe top, skirt, or bodysuit blend into your everyday wardrobe, we recommend adding oversized basics: a blazer, long cardigan, or high-waisted trousers will off-set all those add-ons, but won't lose the vibe.

Folklore Era White Boho Dress

Have a gauzy, dreamy folklore-y dress and not sure how to style it? No problem. You can give even the most ethereal of boho maxi dresses a more grounded feel if you wear it with a denim jacket in a dark or black wash. A vintage handbag and black pumps will keep the look imbued with a vintage-inspired glamour.

evermore Era Floral Gown

Add some edge to the autumnal boho vibes of an evermore era 'fit by incorporating rugged leather accents like belts, vests, or harnesses. You'll know this Eras tour outfit redux has passed the vibe check when it feels more street style than woodland fairy.

Lover Era Pastels

What's pink, blue, and butterflied all over? Swift's Lover era of course. If you bought a bodysuit in this albums iconic palette or DIY-ed your own to match Swift's iconic performance look, you can keep wearing it post-concert by pairing it with a high-waisted denim midi skirt. The denim will tone down the bodysuit's brilliance, while still maintaining an air of romance.

Red Era Sequin Jacket

Who could forget the sequined jackets Swift wore as part of her Red era? Bring this signature Swiftie staple into your regular rotation by pairing it with black separates like a crop top and high-waisted pants.

Fearless Era Metallic Fringe

If you fearlessly channeled your inner Fearless era with a metallic fringe dress or skirt, you've done yourself a major favor. Paired with a thick belt and nude or beige sweater (as seen on Patina Miller, here), Fearless fringe gets an incredibly chic makeover.

1989 Era Hot Pants

Who wears short shorts? A) Taylor Swift in her 1989 era, and also B) you before and after attending the Eras tour. Pair with a half-tucked button-down and Doc Martens, a long-sleeved crop top and stacked sneakers, or an oversized blazer and tank top to give this concert-wear staple an everyday vibe.

Reputation Era Vampy Snake Print

If you wear your Reputation badge of honor proudly, chances are your Eras tour look included one or more snake-patterned or embossed pieces. To bring your reptilian footwear into the everyday, try pairing them with whites and neutrals like this snake boot, khaki skirt, and white tank combo.

Speak Now Era Ballgown

We're not saying a ballgown ever needs to be toned down, but if you did want to rewear a Speak Now Eras concert ballgown, we'd suggest tossing a leather jacket over the look and adding combat boots for a fun night out.

Taylor Swift Era Westernwear

If you went back where it all began, you picked the Swiftian era most suited for daily rewearing. Whether you went for metallic or bedazzled Western separates or opted for a softer, more Coastal Cowgirl look with a pair of statement cowboy boots, all you need are a few pieces with a contrasting aesthetic to make the look feel casual. A graphic tee and cut-off shorts or a simple white crop top and miniskirt will get the job done.
