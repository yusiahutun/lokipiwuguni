Down to the details: Most accurate map ever produced of America win cartography award

Geographer creates the most accurate map of America EVER after spending 6,000 hours on it over two years

In the detail-oriented world of map making, the fact that this year's winner of the best map of America was a man who dedicated his life to the project himself rather than a large company did not go overlooked.

It was a David versus Goliath style victory for David Imus who spent two years coming up with the precise map of the United States.

His approximate 6,000 hours of work earned him the 'Best In Show' award at this year's Cartography and Geographic Information Society.

The victory comes even though Mr Imus' mapping company is essentially a one-man operation working out of his farmhouse near Eugene, Oregon.

The map, created by David Imus, is meant to give a general picture of the demographic spread of the country

To the average viewer, Mr Imus' version is similar to any other map that could adorn the walls of a middle school classroom.

On further inspection, each small decision has an effect on the overall aesthetic.

Opting for accuracy over bold statements, Mr Imus decided to use a thick green line for all of the political borders.

Contrasted to some maps where several different colors are rotated through to help make the shapes of the states 'pop', this decision may make it harder to see the areas included, but it helps the map remain consistent and accurate.

Accuracy: Oregon, mapmaker David Imus' home state, shows the level of detail used throughout, with relief shading and population highlights

The map, which is produced on - wait for it - paper, measures in at four feet by three feet.

The decision to make it a physical map as opposed to one that can be viewed online and zoomed in at a moments notice is in keeping with Mr Imus' vision for the work as a whole.

As noted in an article on Slate, one of his biggest hopes was for the map to give a general sense of the country on the national scale, using relief shading to show topography and yellow highlighter-like markings to population centers so that those clues can be picked up by the viewer at a glance.

Details: Three letter airport codes- like CVG in the upper left corner- are included unlike earlier maps

Another unusual decision was to include short notes about the cultural attractions in each city like Wrigley field and Northwestern University in Chicago.

Overall, the most outstanding aspect of Mr Imus' map is the consistency seen throughout. Unlike the bigger corporations who ship out the final duties on their map projects to fact checkers in India, Mr Imus started and completed the project entirely by himself.
