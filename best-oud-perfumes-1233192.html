All About Oud Fragrance: What Is Oud And Where Is It Sourced From?

Having an Oriental connotation to its glory, the oud or oudh is one of the most precious and rarest raw materials in the world. Also known as agarwood and ‘liquid gold’ the oud is produced when the aquilaria (or agarwood) tree, found in Assam, India, and Bangladesh is infected by a mold called phialophora parasitica. As a defense mechanism, it exudes a dense and resinous fragrant, which becomes the source of extracted oud. The age of the agar tree underlays the value of its oud — the older the better! Other essential aspects like the place of origin and whether it is naturally or synthetically infected also affect the aroma. 

A staple in Indian, Middle-eastern, and Japanese olfactory traditions, the seductive, intense, and woody essence of oud has been around for centuries.  

The complex tang of oud can be described as smoky wood, dampened in the warmth of resinous leather. The dark and intense aroma is potent in the initial notes and beautifully descends into the lingering accents, honeyed with richness. 

With oud at their hearts, the exemplary fragrance houses have delivered the most exotic assortments with unforgettable palettes. Floral, citrussy, musky, vanilla glazed, and sensual burnt blends are a few warm and fuzzy notes to mention. Oud has its way to embrace the varying tastes of its connoisseurs.

    How to Choose Oud Perfumes?

    • Based on the Occasion

    As oud blends with a range of fragrances and notes, it is essential to understand whether the oud is a key essence or a tempering, an underlay in a fragrance. This determines if the perfume will be dark, intense, and musky, or mellowed down with fuzzing ingredients. For instance, oud and warm vanilla is a popular duo that enhances the sweetness of the fragrance. The candy-like, fruity, and floral accents make the oud scent engaging for formal to casual wear, or a daytime pick, especially during hotter months. 

    Whereas, when the oud is uplifted with a spicy scent and stronger notes, like pepper, it arouses a more earthy and sensuous aroma, perfect for evening wear, dinners, or night outs. 

    • Based on How You Apply the Perfume

    If you love showering yourself under the swirling spritzes of perfumes and lather up general dozes of sprays then you can choose a light-hearted oud, as it won’t be overpowering for others. On the other hand, if you prefer a minimal application that feels lightweight, a powerful scent is likely to fit the bill.

    The application can also vary based on the aspects, like spots you may prefer to spritz on, or if it is a spray on your garments; these factors also affect how the scent comes off. If you sweat a lot you might want to pick a stronger fragrance or pair it with a carrier oil to last longer.

    Talking about Oud perfumes, our contributor Viktorija Veltmane, a perfume enthusiast, says, “Oud, otherwise known as agarwood, is a complex fragrance that comes from the resinous heartwood of agarwood trees. The aroma is very rich, woody, and has a very pleasant combination of depth. Oud is ultimately used in many fragrances as a base note and may make the perfume light and airy if used together with citrus or floral accent, while might also make a strong, masculine combination if used together with leather, tobacco or patchouli.”

    How to Use Oud Perfumes?

    On the clean skin, begin by shaking the bottle, as it may be required for scents. Begin spritzing on your desired spots on the body. Some most common areas are your neck and wrist. Make sure you do not rub the perfume on the skin, as it may alter the scent significantly. You can also drizzle perfume in the air and walk into it for an airy, light application.

    FAQ's

    Which type of oudh is best?

    The best oud is the one naturally cultivated from Agarwood trees.

    Which perfume has oud in it?

    All major perfume houses have their own blends of oud available in the market. The musky-woody perfumes may have the essence of oud in it. Check the labels and title of the perfume to find out which fragrance is enriched with oud.

    Do fragrances use synthetic oud?

    As oud is extremely rare and costly to harvest, several perfumers incorporate synthetic oud into the scents.

    What does oud smell like?

    Oud brings a dark, intense, leathery-smoky scent, and warm notes to a fragrance. It can be blended with floral, fruity scents to create a newer fragrance.
