T-Pay validation guide and how to fix common verification issues

The Teachers Service Commission's T-Pay is an online platform that allows teachers registered under the commission to access their payslips online. However, one must register by visiting the nearest TSC county office with their identification documents to use the platform. Unfortunately, the system has faced major security challenges where fraudsters utilise details of legit users to gain access. That is why the commission set up T-Pay validation to protect teachers' data. So, how does it work, and how do you fix common verification issues?

The Teachers Service Commission is one of the biggest employers in Kenya. Considering the massive number of employees, the commission likely has a human resource nightmare regarding remuneration. Fortunately, they created the T-Pay portal to allow teachers to access their payslips online. However, the system hasn't been short of hiccups.

So, any registered user that wants to check payslip online must complete updating their account details. This is an added security layer, but how does it work?

What is T-Pay validation?

Most online accounts, including your email address, offer you an extra layer of security to prevent your information from being accessed by unauthorised persons. That is what T-Pay validation is all about.

The move is meant to protect teachers’ information in line with the Data Protection Act 2019. The added layer of security requires one to provide more information to secure their account, including their passport photo and registered mobile number.

When completing this process, anyone accessing the platform must provide an OTP (one-time-password) on top of their login details (username and password). Then, the code is sent to the registered phone number or email to allow a user to log in.

TSC established this system as a countermeasure to fraud. This now means that even teachers' details are secure, and it would be easy to verify their identities.

What are the requirements for validation?

All registered users must upload crucial documents and provide specific information on the T-Pay portal to protect their data. These include:

  • A passport-size photo: The photo should be clear. Your face shouldn't be covered with anything, including spectacles, headgear, or turbans.
  • Active phone number: The phone number you provide will be crucial in future as it will be utilised in your loan applications and processing. The TSC will also use the number for any official communication.
  • Valid email address: You must provide a valid email address by the Teachers Service Commission, taking the format james@mwalimu.tsc.go.ke.

Teachers were given 30 days to update their information on the portal (from June 9 to July 15, 2022). And heads of institutions are also required to validate details of teachers under their supervision within the stipulated time.

T-Pay validation guide

The TSC TPay verification process is quite straightforward, and the Teachers Service Commission requires teachers to update their details in the following manner:

  • Using your phone or computer’s browser, open the official TSC T-Pay portal.
  • You will be asked to enter your user name and password. You will also be required to complete an arithmetic operation for security reasons. When done, hit the login button.
  • If you have forgotten your password, click the “forgot password” link to reset your TPay validation payslip login details.
  • Once you are logged in, upload your current passport photo. The photo must clearly show your face.
  • Enter and verify your current mobile phone number registered under your name.
  • Once you proceed, the system will send an OTP to your mobile number to validate it.
  • Enter the OTP to validate the mobile number.
  • Once done, click on the validate button to prove that it is your mobile number. It is important to note that the system will only accept a valid code. Remember that you cannot change the number once it has been validated. It is the same number that you will be using during loan applications and approvals.
  • You can also enter and verify your trusted email address.
  • You will also get an OTP to the registered email address, just like you did for the telephone number. The same details will be used during loan applications and approvals and cannot be altered.
  • Once you get the OTP in your mail, please enter it in the spaces provided and click validate.

Heads of institutions, on the other hand, are required to have their profiles verified and approved by the Sub-County Directors (primary institutions) and County Directors (for post-primary institutions), respectively.

According to the Ministry of Education, all heads that will not have completed the verification for teachers under their supervision will not be able to access their T-Pay accounts and will have to activate T-Pay once again.

How to fix common verification issues

Teachers validating their T-Pay data to access their public service payslips securely have faced many problems, mostly email verification. So, what are the common problems, and how can they be solved?

Validation code mismatch

A validation code mismatch is a common problem, and it happens because you have skipped one of the most important parts of uploading a passport photo. Therefore, you need to start by uploading the passport photo according to the requirements and proceed to validate your number.

Uploading passport size less than 5MB

If you get an error that you must upload a passport photo less than 5MB, your file is too large for the system to handle. The portal only handles files less than 5MB, so it would be better to compress your photo or take another one that fits the required file size.

Lacking a TSC email

Only a few teachers created their TSC emails, which means that some will face an issue verifying their email because it is non-existent. However, you can create one easily by using the official Microsoft Office link. You must use your first name, surname, and the last two digits of your TSC number.

Where to access the OTP email

If you don’t know where to access your TSC email, it would be hard for you to verify your email address. To access it, visit office.com and log in using your credentials.

You have not received an OTP email

If you have tried everything else and are still not receiving a verification code in your registered email, you can ask the commission's officials for help. Send an email to tpayverification@tsc.go.ke detailing your issue. Ensure that you attach a scanned copy of your original identification document.
