Kenyan Newspapers Review: List of Luxurious Items Bought by William Ruto, Other Gov't Officials

On Friday, April 5, the newspapers reported how state officials and agencies splurged on luxurious items, contradicting President William Ruto's order to cut spending.

The dailies also delved into the impact of the surging shilling, which has been appreciating against the US dollar in recent weeks.

1. Daily Nation

President William Ruto and other government officials have ignored his directive to cut unnecessary expenditures.

According to the publication, officials at both the national and county levels spent millions of taxpayers' funds on luxurious items, including the following:

  • The house on the hill also purchased heavy-duty branded umbrellas for KSh 3 million, with Rempta Enterprises and Miroz Diesel Services Investments as the contractors.

2. The Standard

The National Council of Churches of Kenya (NCCK) has criticised President William Ruto for implementing policies that hurt Kenyans.

NCCK Nairobi chairman Alphonse Kanga accused the government of introducing painful taxes that have left citizens impoverished.

Kanga cited the Finance Act 2023, which introduced a raft of taxes.

"We have noted with great concern that policies put in place by parliament and county assemblies are choking Kenyans. For instance, the Finance Act has raised taxes to unimaginable levels for traders and consumers," Kanga lamented.

The cleric claimed government officials were accumulating wealth at the expense of wananchi.

3. People Daily

Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) governor Kamau Thugge has revealed Kenya has reduced its public debt by KSh 1 trillion on a strong shilling.

Speaking on Thursday, April 4, after a Monetary Policing Committee meeting, Thugge attributed the strengthening of the local currency to the settlement of Eurobond, which saw the exchange rate drop from 160 to 131against the US dollar.

In January, Kenya's debt stood at over KSh 11 trillion, which has since decreased to KSh 10 trillion.

"That transaction which we did with the Eurobond saved the country huge amounts of debt," the governor stated.

4. The Star

A special forces officer was stabbed to death after an alleged fight with his wife in Baringo county.

Constable Vincent Kiprotich lost his life after he reportedly exchanged bitter words with his wife when he was on leave.

His death was linked to a domestic fight, but the police were investigating the incident.

Kiprotich, who was found lying in a pool of blood, was pronounced dead at Mercy Mission Hospital while receiving treatment.

His wife was arrested in connection with the murder.
