Melissa Stark Biography

Melissa Stark’s date of birth is November 11, 1973, and she was born in Baltimore, Maryland. Her father, Walter Stark, works as an eye surgeon at the renowned Wilmer Eye Clinic at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore, Maryland. Melissa is of white descent and her star sign is Scorpio.

The sports reporter grew up in Baltimore and attended a prestigious women’s preparatory school known as Roland Park Country School. During her college years, she became involved with Kappa Alpha Theta Sorority, which specializes in helping women. Melissa went to the University of Virginia, where she earned a degree in Foreign Policy and Spanish. She spent part of her college years as an active reporter, reporting on football and basketball, usually broadcast on national channels.

Immediately after graduation, she was offered a position with WMAR-TV in 1991 as a news intern working at the assignment desk. From WMAR-TV, the budding sports reporter came to CBS as a news intern, where she presented the CBS evening news together with Dan Rather.

ESPN was her next employer and from 1994 to 1995 she worked as a reporter and production assistant for Virginia Sports Marketing and later became a host for Scholastic Sports America, a weekly show in 1996, a program that allowed Melissa to travel throughout the United States and cover college and high school sports. From 1996 to 2003, she was the host of the ESPN network’s Sports Center. Melissa had the privilege of hosting popular shows such as Sunday NFL Countdown and Outside the Lines, a show that happened to win an Emmy. Her days at ESPN were crowned with coverage of major leagues such as NCAA Basketball and National Hockey League.

In 2000, Melissa moved to ABC Sports, where she worked as a senior reporter for Monday Night Football at ABC Sports. She co-hosted the pilot presentation of ABC’s The Runner in 2001 and was a guest presenter on The View. In 2004 she became part of NBC News and served as a national correspondent for The Today Show and also worked as a host for MSNBC Live. From 2012 until today, she has been working for NFL Network, where she hosts First on The Field. The wealth of experience Melissa has gained throughout her career is amazing, and this has made the sports reporter a veteran in her chosen field.
