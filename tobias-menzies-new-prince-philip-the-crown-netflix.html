Tobias Menzies - Everything you need to know about the new Prince Philip

Netflix have confirmed that Tobias Menzies will replace Matt Smith as Prince Philip in The Crown.

The actor will star opposite Olivia Colman, who takes over the titular role of the Queen from Claire Foy, when the third season of the award-winning show returns.

Smith, along with the rest of the cast, has been a firm favourite with fans. He has been continuously praised for his uncanny depiction of the Duke of Edinburgh so naturally, intrigue into what Menzies will bring to the role when he picks up with Prince Philip in the 1970s is at a high.

Here's what we know about the actor:

Background

The 44-year-old is London-born and bred, his mother was a teacher and his father a producer at the BBC. He studied acting at the prestigious Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) and first sampled the world of acting with parts in Foyle's War, Midsomer Murders and Casualty.

Where you might have seen him before

His best-known roles have been in Game of Thrones (Edmure Tully aka the groom in 'Red Wedding' ) and Outlander, in which he plays Frank Randall, as well as his 18th Century ancestor Jonathan Randall.

In 2014, Menzies starred opposite Maggie Gyllenhaal in BBC miniseries, The Honourable Woman. He's also already worked alongside Colman, having had a part in The Night Manager and Mad Men's Jarad Harris - who played the Queen's father King George VI in the first season of The Crown - in The Terror.

Gyllenhaal and Menzies at a Golden Globes party in 2016

Film wise, he's starred opposite Daniel Craig in Casino Royale as M's assistant and also has a pretty impressive resume when it comes to theatre too. He's performed in The History Boys at the National, King Lear at the Young Vic and The Children's Hour opposite Keira Knightley and Elisabeth Moss in 2011 at the Harold Pinter Theatre in London.

He has also been cast in a BBC adaptation of King Lear alongside Anthony Hopkins and Emma Thompson which will air on the channel later this year.

Getty Images

Moss, Menzies, Knightley and Ellen Burstyn in ’The Children’s Hour’ in 2011

Outside of work

Aside from his passion for acting, Menzies has been vocal in his support of a number of charitable interests. In 2016, he volunteered at the refugee camp in Calais, France and earlier this year read poetry alongside Florence Welch and Ben Whishaw at an event in aid of Help Refugees. In 2016, he, Colman and David Tennant also featured in a short film voicing the 'last words' of prisoners on death row for a campaign for human rights charity Reprieve.

In interviews, Menzies is notoriously private about his personal life. He was rumoured to have been in a brief relationship with Kristen Scott Thomas in 2005/2006.
