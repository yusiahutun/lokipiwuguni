The 10 Best Music Recording Apps for Android

Due to the association of Mac computers with creative people like musicians, you might think that iOS is the only mobile platform suitable for music recording. But that isn't true—Android has caught up rapidly in this department.

Android has some fantastic apps for recording songs. Here's our pick of the 10 best music recording apps for Android.

1. BandLab

Close

Being a complete music creation platform in itself, BandLab is the best recording studio app for Android. That's because it is more than just a music recording app. It is a complete music maker that also lets you edit and remix your music. With a 12-track mixer, a guitar tuner, a looper, and lots of audio samples, it's great for band and musician setups.

However, that doesn't mean it is not a good app for single artists or for more simple use. It records quality audio, while also giving you the option to record multiple tracks.

With 37 million users worldwide, it is a good multi-track recording app. It also has a social media element that can help you find new friends, connect with fellow musicians, or meet your next band.

Download:BandLab (Free, in-app purchases available)

2. Dolby On

Close

If you're looking to transform your phone into a powerful recording device, Dolby On can help you do that with a single tap. You can use it for podcasts, songs, sounds, instruments, or ideas with incredible audio quality while also doing away with background noises and interruptions.

Dolby On is one of the best apps to record songs because it comes with a suite of automatic studio effects that help you enhance and improve your music quality effortlessly. While apps like BandLab can be a little too much, Dolby On is made for recording music and sharing it with the world when you're ready.

Moreover, this app lets you customize your recordings with free audio effects or fine-tune them with Dolby's Dynamic EQ, making it a solid recording studio app.

Download:Dolby On (Free)

3. FL Studio Mobile

FL Studio Mobile is a serious recording studio app that's worth checking out. You can use it to create and save complex and complete multi-track music projects on your Android device, tablet, or Chromebook.

It is versatile and comes with numerous options that help you to create beats, record vocals, and mix and master your projects.

With high-quality loops, synthesizers, and percussions, this studio recording app for Android lets you create the beats or instrumentals you want. It is also an app that lets you record over instrumentals, which makes it one of the best Android apps for music production.

With excellent features like that, FL Studio Mobile is a good app for recording songs. However, its interface can take some getting used to, and not to mention it comes for a steep price.

Download:FL Studio Mobile ($14.99)

4. Voloco

Close

Voloco helps you record better tracks, elevates your sound to professional quality, and is extremely intuitive to use. With over 50 million downloads, it stands out because it lets you create the best songs with more than 50 effects (categorized into 12 preset packs) and thousands of free beats.

Moreover, being one of the best Android apps for musicians, it automatically removes background noise and corrects the pitch of your voice to help you stay in tune. It also offers a variety of presets for EQ, compression, and reverb effects that polish your recording to perfection.

You can also extract vocals in this song recording app. Go ahead and separate them from existing songs to hear what Elvis would sound like with pitch correction.

Download:Voloco (Free, in-app purchases available)

5. Smart Voice Recorder

Close

If you're still wondering which app is the best for recording songs, you can take a look at Smart Voice Recorder, which has been specifically designed for creating high-quality audio using a simple and clean interface.

There's a "skip silence" feature that omits relative silence and awkward pauses, thereby helping shorten your recordings. It has excellent features like a live audio spectrum analyzer, launcher shortcuts, a microphone gain calibration tool, and wave PCM encoding to make recording your songs or your podcasts feel like a dream.

Download:Smart Voice Recorder (Free, in-app purchases available)

6. RecForge II

Close

Being a powerful audio recorder with some advanced features and great sound quality, RecForge II is one of the best song recording apps available. It works with external microphones and offers real-time monitoring, so you can hear what your recording sounds like as you play.

There are basic editing and mixing features, too. You can cut and join tracks, create loops, and easily change the tempo or pitch of your music. It also supports manual gain adjustment and silence skipping, like Smart Recorder.

The app supports a massive range of file formats, including the most common audio formats, and at any quality, so you can share your music without needing to use any other apps.

Download:RecForge II (Free) | RecForge II Pro ($3.99)

7. Music Maker JAM

Close

Music Maker Jam is another solid music app. It makes creating music and mixes easy due to the vast library of mix packs and loops. The only limitation is the app is more geared towards specific music genres, including EDM, Rap, Hip-Hop, House, Pop, Rock, Jazz, and Garage.

So if you want a solid app for creating electronic music, this is a good option. For music creation, an eight-channel mixer is available at your disposal. And as expected of a music recorder, you can sing or rap over the beats you create inside the app.

Download:Music Maker JAM (Free, in-app purchases available)

8. Voice Recorder

Close

With a pretty direct name, Voice Recorder is worth checking out because it is simple to use, offers high-quality recordings, and is completely free. There's more. There are no time limits, so you can record things like lectures, ideas, music, or personal notes for as long as you need to.

What makes it so good is the plethora of features it comes with. From a microphone gain calibration tool to a live audio spectrum analyzer, to recording in the background, it supports it all and more. You can also send and share your recordings easily.

Download:Voice Recorder (Free, in-app purchases available)

9. ASR Voice Recorder

Close

An easy-to-use voice recorder that lets you record audio in a variety of formats, ASR Voice Recorder allows you to record music or other audio through a Bluetooth microphone. It supports high-quality audio and offers easy cloud upload integration with popular cloud service providers like Google Drive and Dropbox.

We don't recommend recording audio via a Bluetooth device like your wireless headphones because it will negatively impact the audio quality. Using a wired headset is better if you want the best possible audio quality.

You can also group your recordings by tags or trim them within the app to use, send, and share as you'd like.

Download:ASR Voice Recorder (Free, in-app purchases available)

10. Easy Voice Recorder

Close

As its name suggests, Easy Voice Recorder is easy to use and works great for simple recordings. It is one of the best apps for recording your songs because you can record them with a single tap, and you can fine-tune them later. You no longer have to go through all the trouble of searching for tutorials on how to record a song on your phone using this app.

Easy Voice Recorder is perfect for all your needs because it has no time limits on a recording. Moreover, it lets you choose a compressed format that also helps you save space on your Android device. If you've deleted a file accidentally, you have the option to restore it too.

Download:Easy Voice Recorder (Free, in-app purchases available) | Easy Voice Recorder Pro ($4.99)

Record Audio and Songs for Later

Android grows as a creative platform all the time. All the music recording apps we talked about let you produce high-quality audio anytime, anywhere. Whether you're taking quick voice notes, making a song for later, or storing your college lectures to listen and learn, these apps will help you in your quest and do so much more.

Do your research, choose one recording app that fits your needs, and then unleash your creativity!
