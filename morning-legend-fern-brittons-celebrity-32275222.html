This Morning legend Fern Britton's Celebrity Big Brother game plan 'exposed'

Fern Britton's "strategy" to survive Celebrity Big Brother has been "exposed".

The 66-year-old television presenter is best known for hosting ITV daytime show This Morning from 1999 until 2009 and since 2010, she has published a number of bestselling novels and books of short stories and non-fiction.

The mother-of-tour recently entered the CBB house as one of the 13 famous housemates but will her career in three-decade long career in television give her an advantage?

Carla Speight, a PR expert and talent manager, told Daily Star: "Fern Britton is a seasoned veteran of TV who’s going to be surprising in the show. She’s going to bring a game plan for sure. She’s been in showbiz long enough to know with this show you need to come prepared and I have no doubt she will have a strategy."

Over the years, Fern has hinted that she felt she was treated badly during her time on the This Morning but she has never spoken out directly about the rumoured feud between herself and former co-host Phillip Schofield, who joined the show n 2002 replacing Fern's previous co-host John Leslie. The pair appeared to get on well on screen, often sharing long giggles together on-screen.

However, it was rumoured that the friendship had turned frosty. Eventually Fern left the show in 2009 after 10 years and was replaced by Holly Willoughby, who had co-hosted with Schofield previously when standing in as holiday and sick cover for Fern. She later said she had felt badly treated on the show. She told RTE radio in 2020: "I began not to enjoy it so much and the morning I resigned, I didn’t know I was going to resign. Something happened and I thought, 'That’s it really' and I walked away and resigned. I was treated pretty poorly, actually. And after a while I just thought, 'Sod it'".

After Fern left the show there were rumours that she had quit over a pay dispute - because she had discovered that Schofield was paid more £250,000 a year more than her for doing the same job. But Britton has always denied this. She told Hello! magazine: "That certainly wasn’t true, not least because I had no idea what Phillip had been paid. I knew what I was being paid and it was more than fair.

"It is appalling that, if you are doing the same job as a man, you are paid less and I am absolutely all for equal pay but at the time, even if Phillip had been paid more than me, he was doing more programmes on television."

Celebrity Big Brother airs Sunday to Friday on ITV1, STV and ITVX at 9pm
