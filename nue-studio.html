Nue Studio is the seasonless, investment partywear label

Each month, we look into an exciting and innovative label that is taking the fashion world by storm in our regular feature #TheBrand. This time, we turn the spotlight on the party-perfect Nué Studio, a label which has become a favourite among A-listers and fashion influencers alike having been seen on everyone from Kendall Jenner to Tiffany Hsu and Pernille Teisbaek – and could not be a better brand to embrace for your after-dark wardrobe.

A post-lockdown world has seen many of us want to dress up like we never have before. After months spent in sweatpants and socialising on Zoom, there has been a movement towards embracing dressing up for no reason at all. At the same time, there has been an awakening regarding sustainability with many consumers now choosing to buy less, but better. This all means that investing in high-end partywear has never been a more appealing prospect, making Nué Studio a great brand to have on your radar.

Launched in Kyiv in 2019, Nué Studio is inspired by Old Hollywood glamour and the roaring Twenties – and is actually described by founder Victoria Udina as “jewellery clothing”.

“It all started with a fabric,” she says. “I discovered it during one of my trips to Italy as a fabric buyer. It was so breath-taking. It made me want to create a small statement piece that was effortless yet eye-catching, sparkling but simple at the same time, and that’s how the whole concept of the brand began.”

Courtesy of Nue Studio

The ethos behind Nué Studio is all about injecting some sparkle and glamour into a timeless outfit. Handcrafted bejewelled bralettes and rhinestone-embellished slinky silk separates make up the bulk of the collection, which is designed to be seasonless. These are core pieces that are created with the best fabrics, and are intended to remain relevant for years to come.

In many ways, the label is tapping into a new feeling of investment that many consumers are embracing more than ever before, buying pieces that they will be able to wear forever, and that are versatile enough to wear for different occasions, rather than spending money on passing trends and pieces that they will only wear once, something which was particularly true with partywear.

“I believe that more and more women are looking for long-lasting pieces in their wardrobes. They want to invest in those items that they would potentially wear again and again, now without any prejudice of repeating outfits.”

Courtesy of Nue Studio

And, while Nué Studio pieces are all certainly show-stopping and party-appropriate, Udina is also designing with versatility in mind, and this is very much how she has seen her customer styling the clothes.

Courtesy of Nue Studio

“Some of the best ways to style our pieces are to mix them with casual outfits. Whether it’s our Spiral blazer counterbalanced by denim, our Charlotte top styled over a T-shirt or our silk shirts mixed with cosy sweaters and combat boots. We are in a new era of jewellery and sparkles – we can now wear high-low mixes such as diamonds with casual outfits or add sparkles to everyday life without feeling overdressed.”

In fact, Udina believes that some of these pieces can truly be considered part of a capsule wardrobe, which is really just about investing in what you are going to wear for a long time.

“You don’t have to follow the standard advice of creating an essential capsule wardrobe – you should add personality, follow your heart. Think about what you want to wear more and what matches most of the pieces you already possess and your lifestyle. You may discover that as well as a white shirt or a black blazer, you want some sparkle as well.”
