Professional Career

NFL Career

In the 2001 NFL draft, the Chicago Bears picked Karon Riley in the fourth round in 103rd overall pick. However, he spent only one year with the Bears in 2001. He left the Bears and joined the Atlanta Falcons. From 2002 to 2004, he played for Falcons.

After that, he went on to play for the Toronto Argonauts in the Canadian Football League in 2005. He then signed a contract with the Washington Redskins in early 2006. The Redskins released him on August 28, 2006, after about eight months.

Finally, in 2007, he joined the Las Vegas/Cleveland Gladiators of the Arena Football League. He left Gladiators in 2008 after only a short time. Moreover, he appeared in 25 games and made seven tackles in the National Football League. In the Arena Football League, he had 8.5 sacks, 6 forced fumbles, and 2 fumble recoveries in 47 games.
