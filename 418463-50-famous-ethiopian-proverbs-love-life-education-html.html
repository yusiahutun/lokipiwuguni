50+ famous Ethiopian proverbs about love, life, and education

African communities are well known for their culture and witty proverbs. Ethiopia is one such country whose proverbs have been common in literature worldwide. While the country is made up of several communities, each one of them has hundreds of proverbs on different aspects of life including wealth, hard work, health, children, and co-existing with others. If you are looking for inspiration or general advice, these Ethiopian proverbs will come in handy.

In ancient times, what made some leaders greater was not just in their lands and titles but how many Ethiopian proverbs they could speak when the situation arises. For them, proverbs were not just words that can convey authority but gifts from nature to guide men on making the best use of their time.

Ethiopian proverbs

The best thing about Ethiopian proverbs is that they are applicable in many different situations and not just the local culture. They provide advice on how to approach different stages of life and solve problems that might leave you in a dilemma. While some of the proverbs may seem obvious, you will be wowed by their deeper meanings once you take a second to think about them.

Famous Ethiopian proverbs

Some of the most famous Ethiopian proverbs have been passed down multiple generations. It’s amazing that the advice they provided back then is still relevant today. They cut across different areas such as religion, co-existence between members of the community, and decision-making.

  • A good name is better than a good perfume.
  • Cow dung cannot be gathered where no cow has been.
  • In the abundance of water, a fool will still be thirsty.
  • The leopard's eye is on the goat, and the eye of the goat is on the leaf.
  • Do not catch a leopard by its tail, but if you do, then do not let it go.
  • It is better to be the cub of a living jackal than that of a dead lion.
  • One who learns will eventually teach.
  • A house with no woman is like a barn with no cow.
  • Counsel and advise him. If he refuses to listen, then let adversity teach him.
  • A rat's witness is another rat.
  • Move your neck according to the music.
  • Those who wish to eat a vulture will call it a guinea fowl.
  • Life has no meaning for one without a home.
  • The best of humanity is a farmer, and the best type of food is fruit.
  • Restless feet may lead you into a snake pit.
  • That which is over-inflated will burst into pieces.
  • A coward will sweat even in water.
  • I've got a cow in the sky but cannot drink her milk.
  • The frog wanting to be as large as the elephant ended up bursting.
  • Telling a secret to an unworthy person is like carrying grain in a bag with a hole in it.
  • No one can know what the new day will bring.
  • Regret, like a tail, comes at the end.
  • Where there is no shame, there cannot be any honour.
