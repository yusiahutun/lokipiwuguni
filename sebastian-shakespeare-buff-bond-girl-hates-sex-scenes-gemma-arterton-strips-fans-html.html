Prince Charles has a very personal and painful reason to attend a ceremony in Dundee this weekend, marking 100 years since the Battle of Loos. 

His great-uncle Fergus, the Queen Mother’s elder brother, was killed in the battle. 

As he led an attack on the German lines, his leg was blown off by a barrage of artillery and he fell back into his sergeant’s arms. 

Bullets struck him in the chest and shoulder and he died on the field. He never saw his only child, Rosemary, who was born two months before his death. 

Fergus’s mother, Cecilia Bowes-Lyon, was severely affected by the loss and became an invalid after his death, withdrawing from public life until the marriage of Elizabeth to the future King George VI in 1923.

 Dundee’s City Square is to be made up like a cathedral for the service, with more than 1,000 people expected to attend.
