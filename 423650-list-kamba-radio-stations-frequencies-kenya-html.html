List of all Kamba radio stations and their frequencies in Kenya

The last few years have seen Kenyan radio stations in Kenya change their communication strategies to appeal to the local communities. As a result, numerous Kamba radio stations have gained popularity in the Eastern region of the country and beyond.

As a media outlet, radio is a preference for many individuals. Compared to others, radio is cheaper and easily accessible even through a mobile phone. For some people, it also has an emotional connection as the listeners interact with their favourite personalities. So, how many Kamba radio stations do we have in Kenya?

Kamba radio stations

The increase in Kamba radio stations has created several job opportunities for Kenyans. The numerous options also provide diverse sources of entertainment. Here is the list of Kamba radio stations:

1. County FM

County FM is one of the leading Kamba radio stations in Kenya located in Kitui, Kenya and broadcasts its programs purely in the Kamba language. Moreover, the 24-hour station broadcasts cultural talk shows and plays a variety of African music. County FM began its operations on September 1, 2013.

The station broadcasts on four licensed frequencies, namely:

  • 90.3 – Kitui
  • 89.2 - Meru
  • 89.5 - Kibwezi
  • 91.8 - Mwingi.

It has reached 11 counties, including Machakos, Tharaka Nithi, Embu, Kajiado, Taita Taveta and Tana River counties.

2. Athiani FM

Athiani FM is one of the top Kamba radio stations. Who owns Athiani FM? Athiani Holdings Limited, a company that aspires to be the leading state-of-the-art media house in Kenya, owns the station. Rumour has it that the company is affiliated with former Vice President Kalonzo Musyoka.

Athiani offers medium entertainment, programmes and information targeting a general audience between 18 and 50 years. The radio station is on the 11th floor of Nairobi's FCB Mirhab Building along Lenana Road in Kilimani.

Athiani FM has frequencies in:

  • Kibwezi (94.7)
  • Machakos (99.2)
  • Kitui (97.7)
  • Meru (99.5)

Listeners can also tune in live through the website. Athiani seeks to connect itself with the aspirations of the Kamba community, offering high-quality radio programming.

3. Mbaitu FM

Mbaitu prides itself as the only FM radio station that mirrors the Kamba community’s hopes and aspirations while creatively showcasing their experiences through the slice of life factor. The Kamba station is in Upperhill, Nairobi, Kenya and has been closely linked to Hon. Charity Ngilu, the Kitui County governor.

You can listen to Mbaitu in Nairobi and Machakos counties on 92.5FM, Mwingi and Kitui counties on 100.4FM and Makueni County on 100.5FM. Fans can also listen to the station live on its website.

4. Musyi FM

Musyi FM operates under the umbrella of the Royal Media Services. The station was birthed on July 25th 2005, making it one of the oldest Kamba radio stations. It broadcasts on 102.2 FM in Nairobi, Mombasa and the greater Lower Eastern Kenya region.

Musyi means an arrow aiming to shoot with a bow, so the station disseminates information fast, protecting the Akamba people from exploitation by empowering them with relevant information and knowledge. Musyi also means home, so listeners feel at home when listening to the station. Fans can also listen to their favourite songs or programs live through the station's website.

5. Syokimau FM

Syokimau FM 88.7 is another Kamba radio station based in Kitui town, Kilungya Street. The station serves the lower Eastern regions. The 24-hour station provides information for the community, talk, news and traditional music and can be streamed live through the website.

6. Mwaki FM

Mwaki FM is another Kamba radio station that is an outfit of Balindiway Media. The station plays music that relaxes the soul, causing listeners to forget their problems. The station is in Nairobi, Kenya, and listeners can stream online to listen to their favourite shows and music.

7. Radio Mang'elete

Radio Mang'elete is the oldest community radio in Kenya started in the 90s with a vision to empower and develop the local community. The station is a project of Mang’elete Community Integrated Development Programme (MCIDP), a community-based organisation.

It broadcasts on the frequency 89.1 in the Kamba language but with intersperses of Kiswahili. The station operates in Nthongoni Shopping Centre, Kibwezi sub-county, Makueni County, and operates daily between 6 am and 10 pm.

8. Mwatu FM

KBC Mwatu FM operates under the ownership of the Kenya Broadcasting Corporation (KBC). It mainly serves people around Machakos town and those along Mombasa Road. Listeners can tune in to the station on 93.1 or listen online.

9. Sang’u FM

Sang'u FM is one of the new entrants into the radio scene. The online radio station is affiliated with KBC Radio and TV host Bonnie Musambi and his wife, Betty Musambi. Sang'u means 'Too much sweetness,'' and the station's tagline is ''Vaa nivo kwaku'’ meaning, ‘'This is where you belong.’'

10. Wikwaty’o FM

Wikwaty’o FM is a Christian faith-based station in Kitui town and whose frequency is 105.3. It is a subsidiary of Adventist world radio and operates under the stewardship of the Seventh Day Adventist Church.

Fans of Kamba music can now listen to their favourite music and programs without any hustle. Thanks to technological advancements, radio lovers can now tune in from the convenience of their hand-held devices. The entertainment scene will likely see more of these stations come up while the existing ones work to expand their reach.
